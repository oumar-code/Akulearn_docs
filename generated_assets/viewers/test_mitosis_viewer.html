Narration Transcript

Cell division through mitosis is essential for growth and repair.

  1. Prophase: The cell begins division. Chromatin condenses into visible chromosomes. The nuclear envelope breaks down, and spindle fibers begin to form from the centrosomes.
  2. Metaphase: Chromosomes align at the cell's equator. Spindle fibers attach to each chromosome at the centromere. The cell pauses here to ensure proper alignment.
  3. Anaphase: Sister chromatids separate and move toward opposite poles of the cell. The spindle fibers shorten, pulling the genetic material apart.
  4. Telophase: Chromosomes arrive at opposite poles. The nuclear envelope reforms around each set of chromosomes. Cytokinesis begins as the cell membrane pinches inward.

The result is two identical daughter cells, each with a complete copy of the genetic information.
