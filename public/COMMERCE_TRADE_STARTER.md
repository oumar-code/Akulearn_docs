## 1. KEY CONCEPTS (Master These)

### Concept 1: Trade – Definition and Types

**Definition**: Trade is the buying and selling of goods and services

**Types of Trade**:

| Type | Definition | Example |
|------|-----------|---------|
| **Home Trade (Internal)** | Trade within a country | Buying garri from a local market |
| **Foreign Trade (External/International)** | Trade between countries | Nigeria exporting crude oil to China |
| **Wholesale Trade** | Buying in large quantities from producers and selling in smaller quantities to retailers | CFAO buying 10,000 units of goods |
| **Retail Trade** | Selling directly to the final consumer in small quantities | A shopkeeper selling one loaf of bread |

**Channels of Distribution**:
```
Producer → Wholesaler → Retailer → Consumer   (Indirect/Long channel)
Producer → Retailer → Consumer                (Shorter channel)
Producer → Consumer                           (Direct selling)
```

**Functions of a Wholesaler**:
1. Buys in bulk from producers
2. Stores goods (warehousing)
3. Breaks bulk (splits into smaller quantities for retailers)
4. Provides credit to retailers
5. Transports goods to retailers
6. Provides market information to producers

**Functions of a Retailer**:
1. Sells in small quantities to consumers
2. Stocks a variety of goods
3. Provides credit to trusted customers
4. Offers after-sales service
5. Provides market information to wholesalers/producers
6. Delivers goods to customers (large retailers)

---

### Concept 2: Types of Retail Trade

| Type | Description | Examples |
|------|-----------|---------|
| **Market stall** | Temporary fixed or mobile point of sale | Balogun Market Lagos |
| **Itinerant trader** | Moves from place to place | Street hawkers, door-to-door salespeople |
| **Small independent shop** | One-person, single-location shop | Local provision store (bukka) |
| **Supermarket** | Large self-service store | Shoprite, Spar |
| **Department store** | Large store divided into specialist departments | UTC, Kingsway stores |
| **Cooperative store** | Owned and managed by members | Farmers' cooperative |
| **Vending machines** | Automated dispensers | Drinks/snacks vending |
| **E-commerce** | Buying and selling via the internet | Jumia, Konga |

**Differences: Wholesale vs Retail**:
| Feature | Wholesale | Retail |
|---------|----------|--------|
| Buyer | Retailer (not final consumer) | Final consumer |
| Quantity | Large (bulk) | Small |
| Price per unit | Lower | Higher |
| Number of customers | Fewer | Many |
| Variety of goods | Usually one or few types | Wide variety |

### Concept 3: Business Documents (Most Tested)

**The Flow of Business Documents in a Trade Transaction**:
```
1. Buyer sends ENQUIRY → to Seller
2. Seller sends QUOTATION (Price List/Catalogue) → to Buyer
3. Buyer sends PURCHASE ORDER → to Seller
4. Seller sends ACKNOWLEDGEMENT → to Buyer
5. Seller sends ADVICE NOTE (goods are coming) → to Buyer
6. Seller delivers goods with DELIVERY NOTE
7. Seller issues INVOICE (request for payment) → to Buyer
8. Buyer checks goods; sends DEBIT NOTE (if overcharged) or CREDIT NOTE received
9. Seller issues STATEMENT OF ACCOUNT (monthly summary)
10. Buyer pays and receives RECEIPT
```

**Key Documents Explained**:

| Document | Sent By | Purpose |
|---------|---------|---------|
| **Enquiry** | Buyer | Asking for price and availability of goods |
| **Quotation** | Seller | Stating price, terms, and conditions |
| **Purchase Order** | Buyer | Formal request to buy specific goods at quoted price |
| **Proforma Invoice** | Seller | Invoice sent BEFORE goods are dispatched (used for import/export, credit checks) |
| **Delivery Note** | Seller | Accompanies goods; buyer signs to confirm receipt |
| **Invoice** | Seller | Bill requesting payment; shows quantity, price, terms |
| **Credit Note** | Seller | Issued when buyer was overcharged or goods returned |
| **Debit Note** | Buyer | Issued when buyer was undercharged |
| **Statement of Account** | Seller | Monthly summary of all transactions; balance owed |
| **Receipt** | Seller | Proof that payment has been received |

### Concept 4: Terms of Trade and Payment Methods

**Common Trade Terms**:
| Term | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **Cash on Delivery (COD)** | Buyer pays when goods are delivered |
| **Credit terms** | Buyer pays after a set period (e.g., 30 days) |
| **Trade discount** | Reduction given to retailers by wholesalers (not shown on invoice net price) |
| **Cash discount** | Reduction for paying early (e.g., 2% for paying within 7 days) |
| **Carriage paid** | Seller pays transport costs |
| **Carriage forward** | Buyer pays transport costs |
| **FOB (Free on Board)** | Seller's responsibility ends when goods are loaded on the ship |
| **CIF (Cost, Insurance, Freight)** | Seller pays cost, insurance, and freight to the destination port |

**Methods of Payment**:
| Method | Description |
|--------|-----------|
| Cash | Physical currency notes and coins |
| Cheque | Written order to bank to pay a specific amount |
| Bank transfer / NEFT | Electronic transfer between bank accounts |
| Draft / Bank draft | Guaranteed payment order issued by a bank |
| Letter of Credit | Bank guarantees payment on behalf of buyer (for imports) |
| Hire Purchase | Buyer pays in instalments; ownership transfers after last payment |

## 2. SAMPLE PROBLEMS (5 Increasing Difficulty)

### Problem 1 (EASY – Types of Trade)
**Q**: Which of the following is an example of home trade?

**Options**:
A) Nigeria exporting crude oil to China
B) A shopkeeper in Abuja selling bread to a customer ← **CORRECT**
C) A UK company importing cocoa from Ghana
D) A company buying computers from the USA

**Explanation**:
- Home trade = trade within the same country
- All parties (buyer and seller) are in Nigeria → home trade
- All other options involve two different countries → foreign trade

**Key Concept**: Home trade = same country; Foreign trade = different countries

---

### Problem 2 (EASY – Business Documents)
**Q**: A document issued by a seller to correct an overcharge on a previous invoice is called a:

**Options**:
A) Debit Note
B) Statement of Account
C) Credit Note ← **CORRECT**
D) Proforma Invoice

**Explanation**:
- Credit Note = issued by the SELLER to REDUCE the amount owed by the buyer (e.g., overcharge, returned goods)
- Debit Note = issued by the BUYER to tell the seller they've been undercharged
- Memory trick: Credit Note → you "credit" the buyer's account (reduce their debt)

**Key Concept**: Credit Note = seller reduces buyer's debt (overcharge/returned goods)

---

### Problem 3 (MEDIUM – Functions)
**Q**: Which of the following is NOT a function of a wholesaler?

**Options**:
A) Breaking bulk
B) Storing goods
C) Selling directly to consumers ← **CORRECT**
D) Providing credit to retailers

**Explanation**:
- Wholesalers sell to RETAILERS, not directly to consumers
- Selling directly to consumers is a function of RETAILERS
- Breaking bulk, storing goods, and providing credit are all genuine wholesaler functions

**Key Concept**: Wholesaler → Retailer (not direct to consumer)

---

### Problem 4 (MEDIUM – Trade Terms)
**Q**: A trade discount is:

**Options**:
A) A reduction given to a customer who pays cash immediately
B) A reduction given to retailers by wholesalers based on the quantity purchased ← **CORRECT**
C) A discount given for damaged goods
D) Reduction on goods that are about to expire

**Explanation**:
- Trade discount = a reduction off the catalogue/list price given to trade buyers (retailers, wholesalers)
- It is NOT shown on the invoice (invoice shows the discounted net price)
- Cash discount = reduction for prompt payment; different concept

**Key Concept**: Trade discount = for being in the trade (bulk buyer); Cash discount = for paying quickly

---

### Problem 5 (HARD – Document Flow)
**Q**: Arrange these business documents in the correct order of a trade transaction:
1. Invoice, 2. Delivery Note, 3. Purchase Order, 4. Quotation, 5. Receipt

**Options**:
A) 4, 3, 2, 1, 5 ← **CORRECT**
B) 3, 4, 1, 2, 5
C) 4, 3, 1, 2, 5
D) 3, 4, 2, 1, 5

**Explanation**:
```
Step 1: Seller sends QUOTATION (price list) → to buyer
Step 2: Buyer sends PURCHASE ORDER → to seller
Step 3: Seller delivers goods with DELIVERY NOTE
Step 4: Seller sends INVOICE (bill for payment)
Step 5: Buyer pays → Seller issues RECEIPT
```

**Key Concept**: Quotation → Order → Delivery Note → Invoice → Receipt

## 3. COMMON MISTAKES IN COMMERCE

| Mistake | Why Wrong | Correct Understanding |
|---------|-----------|------------------------|
| Confusing Credit Note and Debit Note | Both adjust accounts | Credit Note = seller issues (buyer overcharged); Debit Note = buyer issues (undercharged) |
| Saying wholesaler sells to consumers | Wholesaler ↔ Retailer | Wholesaler sells to retailers; Retailer sells to consumers |
| Confusing trade discount with cash discount | Both are "discounts" | Trade = for being a trade buyer; Cash = for prompt payment |
| Thinking an invoice IS a receipt | Both are payment-related | Invoice = request for payment; Receipt = proof of payment received |
| Confusing Proforma Invoice with regular Invoice | Both look similar | Proforma = BEFORE goods are sent (no binding order yet); Invoice = AFTER delivery |

## 5. EXAM QUICK TIPS

**Tip 1**: **Document direction: who sends what to whom**
- Buyer sends: Enquiry, Purchase Order, Debit Note
- Seller sends: Quotation, Invoice, Credit Note, Statement, Receipt

**Tip 2**: **Credit Note reduces buyer's debt; Debit Note increases it**
- Credit Note = goods returned or overcharge → buyer owes LESS
- Debit Note = undercharge discovered → buyer owes MORE

**Tip 3**: **Trade discount ≠ Cash discount**
- Trade = for the trade relationship (bulk); not recorded on invoice as discount
- Cash = for paying early (2/7 net 30 means 2% off if paid within 7 days)

**Tip 4**: **Wholesaler breaks bulk; retailer sells to final consumer**
- Wholesaler does NOT sell to consumers (in traditional channel)

**Tip 5**: **Document order: Quotation → Order → Delivery → Invoice → Receipt**
- This is the most commonly tested sequence in WAEC/JAMB

## 6. WHAT TO MEMORIZE

**MUST MEMORIZE**:
```
Channel: Producer → Wholesaler → Retailer → Consumer

Wholesaler Functions:
  Bulk buying, Break bulk, Storage (warehousing), Credit to retailers, Transport

Retailer Functions:
  Small quantity sales, Variety, Credit to customers, After-sales service

Document Order:
  Enquiry → Quotation → Purchase Order → Delivery Note → Invoice → Receipt

Credit Note: Seller → reduces buyer's debt (overcharge/return)
Debit Note:  Buyer → increases seller's claim (undercharge)
Proforma Invoice: sent BEFORE goods are dispatched

Trade discount = bulk/trade relationship (not on invoice)
Cash discount  = prompt payment reward
```

**Pattern 1**: "Which document is issued by the seller to reduce the amount owed by the buyer?"

**Pattern 2**: "A document sent by a seller before dispatching goods to request payment in advance"
- Proforma Invoice

**Pattern 3**: "Which of the following is NOT a function of the wholesaler?"
- Selling directly to consumers (that is the retailer's function)

**Pattern 4**: "Arrange the following documents in the correct order"
- Enquiry → Quotation → Order → Delivery Note → Invoice → Receipt

**Pattern 5**: "Trade discount differs from cash discount in that trade discount is…"
- Given to trade buyers (retailers/wholesalers) based on relationship/volume, not payment speed
