## 1. KEY CONCEPTS (Master These)

### Concept 1: Physical Quantities and Units

**Definition**: A physical quantity is any property of matter that can be measured

**Two categories**:
| Type | Definition | Examples |
|------|-----------|---------|
| Fundamental (Base) | Cannot be derived from others | Length (m), Mass (kg), Time (s), Temperature (K), Current (A) |
| Derived | Made by combining fundamental units | Speed (m/s), Force (N = kg·m/s²), Energy (J = kg·m²/s²) |

**The SI System** (Système International):
```
Length      → metre (m)
Mass        → kilogram (kg)
Time        → second (s)
Temperature → kelvin (K)
Current     → ampere (A)
Amount      → mole (mol)
Luminosity  → candela (cd)
```

**Common Prefixes**:
| Prefix | Symbol | Multiplier |
|--------|--------|-----------|
| kilo | k | × 10³ |
| centi | c | × 10⁻² |
| milli | m | × 10⁻³ |
| micro | μ | × 10⁻⁶ |

**Why Important**: Every Physics calculation uses units – wrong units = wrong answer

---

### Concept 2: Scalars and Vectors

**Scalar**: Has magnitude (size) only
**Vector**: Has both magnitude AND direction

| Scalar | Vector |
|--------|--------|
| Distance | Displacement |
| Speed | Velocity |
| Mass | Weight |
| Temperature | Force |
| Energy | Acceleration |
| Time | Momentum |

**Key Distinction**:
- Distance = total path travelled (scalar)
- Displacement = shortest straight-line path from start to end (vector)
- Speed = distance / time (scalar)
- Velocity = displacement / time (vector)

**Vector Addition** (Triangle/Parallelogram Rule):
```
For vectors at right angles:
Resultant R = √(A² + B²)     (Pythagoras theorem)

Direction θ = tan⁻¹(B/A)
```

**Example**: A boy walks 3 m east and 4 m north.
- Resultant displacement = √(3² + 4²) = √25 = 5 m
- Direction = tan⁻¹(4/3) = 53° north of east

### Concept 3: Measurement – Instruments and Errors

**Common Measuring Instruments**:
| Quantity | Instrument | Range / Precision |
|---------|-----------|-----------------|
| Length | Metre rule | ± 0.1 cm |
| Length (small) | Vernier calliper | ± 0.01 cm |
| Length (tiny) | Micrometer screw gauge | ± 0.001 cm |
| Mass | Beam balance / Electronic balance | g or kg |
| Time | Stopwatch / Ticker-tape timer | seconds |
| Temperature | Thermometer | °C or K |

**Types of Errors**:
- **Systematic error**: Same error repeated (e.g., zero error on instrument) → offset all readings the same way
- **Random error**: Unpredictable variation → reduced by taking multiple readings and averaging

**Zero Error**: When instrument reads non-zero with no measurement
- Positive zero error → subtract from reading
- Negative zero error → add to reading

**Accuracy vs Precision**:
- Accuracy: How close to the true value
- Precision: How consistent the readings are (can be precise but not accurate)

### Concept 4: Basic Motion – Distance, Speed, Velocity, Acceleration

**Key Formulas**:
```
Speed (s) = Distance (d) / Time (t)
           s = d/t

Velocity (v) = Displacement / Time

Acceleration (a) = Change in velocity / Time
                   a = (v - u) / t
```

**Distance–Time Graph**:
```
Slope = Speed (steeper = faster)
Horizontal line = stationary (at rest)
Curved line = acceleration or deceleration
```

**Velocity–Time Graph**:
```
Slope = Acceleration (steeper = greater acceleration)
Horizontal line = constant velocity (zero acceleration)
Area under graph = Distance travelled
```

**Uniform vs Non-Uniform Motion**:
- Uniform: equal distances in equal times (constant speed)
- Non-uniform: varying speed (acceleration or deceleration)

## 2. SAMPLE PROBLEMS (5 Increasing Difficulty)

### Problem 1 (EASY – Scalar vs Vector)
**Q**: Which of the following is a vector quantity?

**Options**:
A) Temperature
B) Mass
C) Velocity ← **CORRECT**
D) Distance

**Explanation**:
- Velocity has both magnitude (speed) and direction
- Temperature, mass, and distance are scalars (magnitude only)
- Common trick: "Speed" is scalar; "Velocity" is vector

**Key Concept**: Vectors have direction; scalars do not

---

### Problem 2 (EASY – Unit Conversion)
**Q**: Convert 5 km to metres.

**Options**:
A) 0.005 m
B) 500 m
C) 5,000 m ← **CORRECT**
D) 50,000 m

**Solution**:
```
1 km = 1,000 m
5 km = 5 × 1,000 = 5,000 m
```

**Key Concept**: kilo (k) = × 10³

---

### Problem 3 (MEDIUM – Speed Calculation)
**Q**: A car travels 120 km in 2 hours. What is its average speed in m/s?

**Options**:
A) 60 m/s
B) 240 m/s
C) 16.7 m/s ← **CORRECT**
D) 33.3 m/s

**Solution**:
```
First convert: 120 km = 120,000 m; 2 hours = 7,200 s

Speed = Distance / Time
Speed = 120,000 / 7,200
Speed = 16.7 m/s
```

**Key Concept**: Always convert to SI units (m, s) before calculating

---

### Problem 4 (MEDIUM – Resultant Vector)
**Q**: A body moves 6 m east and 8 m north. What is the magnitude of the resultant displacement?

**Options**:
A) 14 m
B) 2 m
C) 10 m ← **CORRECT**
D) 48 m

**Solution**:
```
Resultant = √(6² + 8²)
          = √(36 + 64)
          = √100
          = 10 m
```

**Key Concept**: Use Pythagoras for perpendicular vectors

---

### Problem 5 (HARD – Velocity–Time Graph Analysis)
**Q**: A velocity–time graph shows a straight line from v = 0 at t = 0 to v = 20 m/s at t = 4 s, then a horizontal line from t = 4 s to t = 10 s. What is the total distance travelled?

**Options**:
A) 80 m
B) 120 m
C) 160 m ← **CORRECT**
D) 200 m

**Solution**:
```
Phase 1 (acceleration, t = 0 to 4 s):
Area = ½ × base × height = ½ × 4 × 20 = 40 m

Phase 2 (constant velocity, t = 4 to 10 s):
Area = base × height = 6 × 20 = 120 m

Total distance = 40 + 120 = 160 m
```

**Key Concept**: Area under velocity–time graph = distance travelled

## 3. COMMON MISTAKES IN PHYSICS FOUNDATIONS

| Mistake | Why Wrong | Correct Understanding |
|---------|-----------|------------------------|
| Confusing distance and displacement | Both sound like "how far" | Distance = total path; Displacement = straight-line from start to end |
| Confusing speed and velocity | Same numeric value sometimes | Speed is scalar; velocity is vector (includes direction) |
| Forgetting to convert units to SI | Getting answers with wrong units | Always convert km→m and hours→s first |
| Treating weight as mass | "I weigh 60 kg" | Mass in kg; Weight = mg in Newtons |
| Reading V-T graph slope as distance | Slope = acceleration, area = distance | Slope of V-T = acceleration; area under V-T = distance |

## 5. EXAM QUICK TIPS

**Tip 1**: **Memorise scalar vs vector pairs**
- Distance/Displacement; Speed/Velocity; Mass/Weight; Temperature/Force

- Convert km to m (× 1000) and hours to s (× 3600) before any calculation

- R = √(A² + B²) when two vectors are at 90°

- Distance–Time: slope = speed; flat line = rest

- Positive zero error → subtract from every reading
- Negative zero error → add to every reading

## 8. REAL WAEC/JAMB PHYSICS FOUNDATIONS PATTERNS

**Pattern 1**: "Which of the following is a vector/scalar?"
- Know all standard pairs: Speed/Velocity, Distance/Displacement, Mass/Weight

**Pattern 2**: "Convert [unit] to SI unit"
- Apply prefix multipliers; km→m, g→kg, cm→m

**Pattern 3**: "Calculate the resultant of two perpendicular vectors"
- Use R = √(A² + B²)

**Pattern 4**: "From the velocity–time graph, calculate the distance/acceleration"
- Distance = area; Acceleration = slope

**Pattern 5**: "A car travels … at … km/h. Find speed in m/s"
- Divide km/h by 3.6 to get m/s
