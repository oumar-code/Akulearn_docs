## 1. KEY CONCEPTS (Master These)

### Concept 1: Wave Properties and Definitions

**Definition**: A wave is a disturbance that transfers energy from one point to another without transferring matter

**Key Wave Properties**:

| Property | Symbol | Definition | Unit |
|----------|--------|-----------|------|
| Amplitude | A | Maximum displacement from rest position | metres (m) |
| Wavelength | λ (lambda) | Distance of one complete wave cycle | metres (m) |
| Frequency | f | Number of complete waves per second | Hertz (Hz) |
| Period | T | Time for one complete wave cycle | seconds (s) |
| Wave speed | v | Distance covered per unit time | m/s |

**Key Relationship**:
```
v = fλ

Wave speed = Frequency × Wavelength

Also: T = 1/f  (Period = 1 / Frequency)
```

**Why Important**: 5-7 WAEC/JAMB questions per exam on wave properties and calculations

---

### Concept 2: Types of Waves

**By Direction of Vibration**:

**Transverse Waves**:
- Vibration is PERPENDICULAR to direction of wave travel
- Examples: Light, radio waves, water waves, all EM waves
```
Direction of travel →
   ∿∿∿∿∿∿∿∿∿∿∿∿
↑ vibration is up/down (perpendicular)
```

**Longitudinal Waves**:
- Vibration is PARALLEL to direction of wave travel
- Examples: Sound waves, P-waves (earthquake)
- Consist of compressions (high pressure) and rarefactions (low pressure)
```
Direction of travel →
|||  |||  |||  |||
Compressions and rarefactions along the same axis
```

**Memory Trick**: Trans**verse** → **V**ibration perpendicular | **L**ongitudinal → **L**ine of travel = vibration direction

---

### Concept 3: Sound Waves

**Nature of Sound**:
- Longitudinal wave
- Requires a medium to travel (cannot travel through vacuum)
- Speed in air ≈ 340 m/s at room temperature
- Speed in solids > liquids > gases

**Properties of Sound**:
| Property | Determined By | Description |
|----------|--------------|-------------|
| Pitch | Frequency | High frequency = high pitch |
| Loudness | Amplitude | Large amplitude = loud sound |
| Quality/Timbre | Waveform | Different instruments sound different |

**Resonance**: When a body vibrates at its natural frequency due to an external wave of the same frequency

**Echo**: Reflection of sound; requires minimum distance of ~17 m for an echo to be heard distinctly

---

### Concept 4: Light Waves and Electromagnetic Spectrum

**Nature of Light**:
- Transverse wave
- Does NOT require a medium (travels through vacuum)
- Speed in vacuum: c = 3 × 10⁸ m/s

**Electromagnetic Spectrum** (longest → shortest wavelength):
```
Radio → Microwave → Infrared → Visible → Ultraviolet → X-rays → Gamma rays
  ↑                                                                    ↑
Longest wavelength                                           Shortest wavelength
Lowest frequency                                           Highest frequency
```

**Visible Light** (ROYGBIV):
Red → Orange → Yellow → Green → Blue → Indigo → Violet
(Red has longest wavelength; Violet has shortest)

**Refraction of Light**:
```
Snell's Law: n₁ sin θ₁ = n₂ sin θ₂
or:  n = sin i / sin r  (for air to medium)
```

Where n = refractive index, i = angle of incidence, r = angle of refraction

## 2. SAMPLE PROBLEMS (5 Increasing Difficulty)

### Problem 1 (EASY – Wave Property)
**Q**: A wave has a frequency of 50 Hz and a wavelength of 4 m. What is the speed of the wave?

**Options**:
A) 12.5 m/s
B) 54 m/s
C) 200 m/s ← **CORRECT**
D) 0.0125 m/s

**Solution**:
```
v = fλ
v = 50 Hz × 4 m
v = 200 m/s
```

**Key Concept**: v = fλ is the fundamental wave equation

---

### Problem 2 (EASY – Wave Type)
**Q**: Sound is classified as a longitudinal wave because:

**Options**:
A) It travels at a very high speed
B) It can travel through vacuum
C) Its particles vibrate parallel to the direction of travel ← **CORRECT**
D) It has a very short wavelength

**Explanation**:
- Longitudinal = vibrations parallel to wave direction
- Sound creates compressions and rarefactions in air
- Transverse = vibrations perpendicular to wave direction
- Sound CANNOT travel in vacuum (needs a medium)

**Key Concept**: Longitudinal = parallel vibration; Transverse = perpendicular vibration

---

### Problem 3 (MEDIUM – Calculation)
**Q**: A radio station broadcasts at a frequency of 100 MHz. What is the wavelength of the radio wave? (Speed of light = 3 × 10⁸ m/s)

**Options**:
A) 0.3 m
B) 3 m ← **CORRECT**
C) 30 m
D) 300 m

**Solution**:
```
v = fλ  →  λ = v/f
f = 100 MHz = 100 × 10⁶ Hz = 10⁸ Hz

λ = (3 × 10⁸) / (10⁸)
λ = 3 m
```

**Key Concept**: Convert MHz to Hz (×10⁶); then use λ = v/f

---

### Problem 4 (MEDIUM – Refraction)
**Q**: A ray of light passes from air into glass. The angle of incidence is 45° and the angle of refraction is 30°. Find the refractive index of the glass.

**Options**:
A) 0.71
B) 1.22
C) 1.41 ← **CORRECT**
D) 1.73

**Solution**:
```
n = sin i / sin r
n = sin 45° / sin 30°
n = (√2/2) / (1/2)
n = (0.707) / (0.5)
n = 1.414 ≈ 1.41
```

**Key Concept**: Snell's Law: n = sin i / sin r

---

### Problem 5 (HARD – Combined Application)
**Q**: A sound wave in air at 20°C has a frequency of 680 Hz. If the speed of sound is 340 m/s, how many complete wavelengths fit in a distance of 5 metres?

**Options**:
A) 2
B) 5
C) 8
D) 10 ← **CORRECT**

**Solution**:
```
Step 1: Find wavelength
λ = v/f = 340/680 = 0.5 m

Step 2: Number of wavelengths in 5 m
n = distance / wavelength = 5 / 0.5 = 10
```

**Key Concept**: Number of wavelengths = total distance ÷ one wavelength

## 3. COMMON MISTAKES IN WAVES

| Mistake | Why Wrong | Correct Understanding |
|---------|-----------|------------------------|
| Confusing frequency and period | Using f where T is needed | T = 1/f; Period is in seconds, frequency is in Hz |
| Forgetting to convert MHz/kHz | Getting wrong answer in calculation | 1 MHz = 10⁶ Hz; 1 kHz = 10³ Hz |
| Mixing transverse and longitudinal | Applying wrong properties | Sound = longitudinal; Light = transverse |
| Wrong Snell's Law application | Swapping sin i and sin r | n = sin(angle in air) / sin(angle in medium) |
| Thinking sound travels in vacuum | Confusing sound and light | Sound needs a medium; light does not |

## 5. EXAM QUICK TIPS

**Tip 1**: **Wave equation v = fλ** – rearrange for f or λ when needed:
- f = v/λ
- λ = v/f

**Tip 2**: **Period and Frequency**
- T = 1/f → Period = 1 ÷ Frequency
- If f = 50 Hz, T = 1/50 = 0.02 s

**Tip 3**: **Sound vs Light**
- Sound = longitudinal, needs medium, slower (~340 m/s in air)
- Light = transverse, no medium needed, faster (3×10⁸ m/s)

**Tip 4**: **Refraction direction rule**
- Light going from less dense → more dense: bends TOWARD normal (r < i)
- Light going from more dense → less dense: bends AWAY from normal (r > i)

**Tip 5**: **EM spectrum order from memory**
- "**R**ather **M**ighty **I**nteresting **V**istas **U**nder **X** **G**uns"
- Radio, Microwave, Infrared, Visible, Ultraviolet, X-ray, Gamma
