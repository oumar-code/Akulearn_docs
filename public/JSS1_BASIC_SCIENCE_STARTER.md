## 1. KEY CONCEPTS (Master These)

### Concept 1: Characteristics of Living Things

**All living things share 7 characteristics** — use the acronym **MRS GREN**:

| Letter | Characteristic | What It Means | Example |
|--------|----------------|--------------|---------|
| **M** | Movement | Ability to move part or all of body | Plants grow toward light; animals walk |
| **R** | Respiration | Release of energy from food | Breathing; cellular respiration |
| **S** | Sensitivity | Respond to the environment | Eye blinks in bright light |
| **G** | Growth | Permanent increase in size/complexity | A child growing taller |
| **R** | Reproduction | Produce offspring | Laying eggs; giving birth |
| **E** | Excretion | Remove waste products | Sweating, urinating |
| **N** | Nutrition | Taking in food for energy | Eating; photosynthesis in plants |

**Living vs Non-living**:
- Living: goat, tree, bacteria, mushroom
- Non-living: stone, water, sand, chair (never had life)
- Once-living: charcoal, dried leaf, leather (had life before)

---

### Concept 2: Classification of Living Things

**Five Kingdoms** (simplified for JSS):
| Kingdom | Description | Examples |
|---------|------------|---------|
| Monera | Single-celled, no nucleus | Bacteria |
| Protista | Single-celled, with nucleus | Amoeba, paramecium |
| Fungi | Feed on dead matter, spores | Mushroom, mould |
| Plantae | Make own food (photosynthesis) | Grass, mango tree |
| Animalia | Eat other organisms | Goat, eagle, fish |

**Vertebrates vs Invertebrates**:
- **Vertebrates** — have a backbone: Fish, Amphibians, Reptiles, Birds, Mammals
- **Invertebrates** — no backbone: Insects, worms, crabs, snails, jellyfish

**FARM-B** (Vertebrate groups):
```
F — Fish      (cold-blooded, scales, gills, lay eggs in water)
A — Amphibians (cold-blooded, moist skin, live on land and in water)
R — Reptiles  (cold-blooded, dry scaly skin, lay eggs on land)
M — Mammals   (warm-blooded, hair/fur, give birth to live young, nurse young)
B — Birds     (warm-blooded, feathers, lay hard-shelled eggs)
```

### Concept 3: States of Matter

**Three States of Matter**:
| State | Definite Shape? | Definite Volume? | Example |
|-------|----------------|-----------------|---------|
| Solid | ✅ Yes | ✅ Yes | Ice, stone, iron |
| Liquid | ❌ No (takes shape of container) | ✅ Yes | Water, palm oil |
| Gas | ❌ No | ❌ No | Air, steam, oxygen |

**Changes of State**:
```
Solid → Liquid : Melting       (e.g., ice → water at 0°C)
Liquid → Solid : Freezing      (e.g., water → ice)
Liquid → Gas   : Evaporation / Boiling  (e.g., water → steam at 100°C)
Gas → Liquid   : Condensation  (e.g., steam → water droplets)
Solid → Gas    : Sublimation   (e.g., dry ice → CO₂ gas, camphor)
Gas → Solid    : Deposition    (e.g., frost forming)
```

**Melting Point of water**: 0°C | **Boiling Point of water**: 100°C (at sea level)

**Particle Theory**:
- Solid: particles tightly packed, vibrate in fixed positions
- Liquid: particles close, can slide past each other
- Gas: particles far apart, move randomly at high speed

### Concept 4: Mixtures and Separation Methods

**Types of Mixtures**:
- **Homogeneous** (uniform throughout): salt dissolved in water (solution)
- **Heterogeneous** (not uniform): sand and water, salad

**Separation Techniques**:
| Method | Used For | Example |
|--------|----------|---------|
| Filtration | Solid from liquid | Sand from water |
| Evaporation | Salt from solution | Salt from salt water |
| Distillation | Two miscible liquids with different boiling points | Alcohol and water |
| Decanting | Insoluble solid from liquid (settle first) | Muddy water |
| Magnetic attraction | Magnetic metal from non-magnetic mixture | Iron filings from sand |
| Sieving | Solids of different sizes | Sieving flour |
| Chromatography | Dyes / pigments | Separating ink colours |

---

### Concept 5: Safety in the Science Laboratory

**Laboratory Rules**:
1. Never eat or drink in the lab
2. Always wear safety goggles and protective clothing
3. Never mix chemicals without instruction
4. Know the location of fire extinguisher and first-aid box
5. Report accidents immediately
6. Wash hands thoroughly after experiments
7. Never smell a chemical by bringing it directly to your nose — waft it

**Safety Symbols** (must recognise):
| Symbol | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| Skull and crossbones | Toxic/Poisonous |
| Flame | Flammable |
| Exclamation mark | Irritant/Harmful |
| Test tube with hand | Corrosive |
| Environmental tree | Harmful to environment |

**First Aid for Lab Accidents**:
- Chemical on skin → flush with lots of water immediately
- Fire on clothing → STOP, DROP, ROLL
- Glass cut → apply pressure, do not remove glass, call for help

## 2. SAMPLE PROBLEMS (5 Increasing Difficulty)

### Problem 1 (EASY — Living Things)
**Q**: Which of the following is a characteristic of living things?
A) Melting point   B) Respiration   C) Hardness   D) Solubility

**Answer**: **B — Respiration**

**Explanation**: Respiration (releasing energy from food) is one of the MRS GREN characteristics of living things. The others are properties of matter.

---

### Problem 2 (EASY — States of Matter)
**Q**: Which state of matter has a definite shape and definite volume?

**Answer**: **Solid**

**Explanation**: Solids have fixed shape because particles are tightly packed; liquids take the shape of their container; gases have neither fixed shape nor volume.

---

### Problem 3 (MEDIUM — Classification)
**Q**: A frog is warm-blooded, lays eggs in water and breathes with both lungs and gills at different life stages. Which vertebrate group does it belong to?

**Answer**: **Amphibia (Amphibians)**

**Explanation**: Frogs are cold-blooded (not warm-blooded), live both in water and on land, and undergo metamorphosis. The question tests careful reading — "cold-blooded" is a key identifier.

---

### Problem 4 (MEDIUM — Separation)
**Q**: A student has a mixture of iron filings, salt and sand. List the steps to separate all three components.

**Answer**:
1. Pass a magnet over the mixture → remove **iron filings**
2. Add water to the remaining mixture; stir well → salt dissolves
3. Filter → remove **sand** (residue)
4. Evaporate the salt solution → recover **salt**

---

### Problem 5 (HARD — Changes of State)
**Q**: Explain why a glass of cold water appears to "sweat" on a hot day.

**Answer**: The water vapour in the warm air (gas) loses energy when it touches the cold glass surface and condenses back into liquid water droplets on the outside of the glass. This process is called **condensation**.

## 3. COMMON MISTAKES IN JSS1 BASIC SCIENCE

| Mistake | Correction |
|---------|-----------|
| "Breathing is the same as respiration" | Breathing (inhaling/exhaling) is the physical process; respiration is the chemical release of energy from food |
| Confusing once-living with non-living | Once-living things (charcoal, leather) had life; non-living (stone) never did |
| Saying birds are reptiles because both lay eggs | Birds are warm-blooded with feathers; reptiles are cold-blooded with scales |
| Using filtration to separate salt from water | Filtration removes insoluble solids; salt is soluble → use evaporation |
| Mixing up melting point and boiling point | Melting = solid → liquid (0°C for water); boiling = liquid → gas (100°C for water) |

## 5. EXAM QUICK TIPS

**Tip 1**: **Memorise MRS GREN** — every living things question tests at least one of these

**Tip 2**: **FARM-B** — use this to classify vertebrates quickly

**Tip 3**: **Separation questions** — ask yourself: Is the solid soluble or insoluble? → choose filtration (insoluble) or evaporation (soluble)

**Tip 4**: **Safety questions** — always choose the option that protects people first (water for chemical spills, STOP/DROP/ROLL for fire)

**Tip 5**: **Changes of state** — Solid→Liquid = melting (not dissolving); Liquid→Gas = evaporating/boiling
