## 1. KEY CONCEPTS BY CLASS LEVEL

### JSS1: The Family and the Community

**The Family** — the basic unit of society:
| Family Type | Definition | Example |
|-------------|-----------|---------|
| Nuclear family | Parents and their biological children | Father, mother, children |
| Extended family | Nuclear family plus relatives | Grandparents, aunts, uncles, cousins |
| Monogamous | One man married to one woman | Most Christian families in Nigeria |
| Polygamous | One man married to more than one woman | Practised in some cultures |
| Single-parent | One parent raises children alone | — |

**Functions of the family**:
1. Procreation (giving birth and raising children)
2. Socialisation (teaching values, culture, language)
3. Economic support (providing food, shelter, clothing)
4. Emotional support and protection
5. Education and transmission of culture

**Socialisation**: process by which an individual learns the norms, values and behaviours of their society
- **Primary socialisation**: family (first and most influential)
- **Secondary socialisation**: school, peers, church/mosque, media

**The Community**: people living in the same area who share common interests and resources
- Rural community: farming-based, close-knit, traditional
- Urban community: industrial/commercial, diverse, fast-paced

---

### JSS2: Culture, Values and Social Problems

**Culture**: the total way of life of a people — their beliefs, customs, language, art, food, clothing, traditions

**Elements of Nigerian culture**:
- Language (500+ languages in Nigeria; English as official language)
- Religion (Christianity, Islam, Traditional)
- Arts (Nok terracotta, Benin bronze, Aso-oke, Adire)
- Food (jollof rice, egusi soup, tuwo shinkafa, ukwa)
- Festivals (Eyo, Sallah, New Yam, Argungu fishing festival)

**Cultural practices — positive vs negative**:
| Positive | Negative (harmful) |
|---------|-------------------|
| Respect for elders | Female genital mutilation |
| Communal spirit | Child marriage |
| Hospitality | Forced marriage |
| Extended family support | Trial by ordeal |

**Social problems in Nigeria**:
| Problem | Causes | Effects | Solutions |
|---------|--------|---------|-----------|
| Unemployment | Rapid population growth, poor education | Poverty, crime | Vocational training, SME support |
| Poverty | Unemployment, corruption, unequal distribution | Poor health, crime, hunger | Social welfare, equitable distribution |
| Drug abuse | Peer pressure, unemployment, stress | Crime, health problems | Education, rehabilitation |
| Corruption | Greed, weak institutions | Underdevelopment, poverty | Accountability, anti-corruption laws |
| Crime | Poverty, unemployment, moral decadence | Insecurity, loss of life | Law enforcement, economic development |

### JSS3: Government, Democracy and Citizenship

**Government**: the institution responsible for managing the affairs of a state/country

**Types of government**:
| Type | Definition | Example |
|------|-----------|---------|
| Democracy | Government by the people (through elected representatives) | Nigeria, USA |
| Monarchy | Rule by a king/queen | Saudi Arabia, UK (constitutional) |
| Theocracy | Rule based on religious laws | Iran |
| Oligarchy | Rule by a small group | Military junta |
| Dictatorship | Rule by one person with absolute power | North Korea |

**Democracy — key principles**:
1. Popular sovereignty (power belongs to the people)
2. Rule of law (everyone equal before the law)
3. Separation of powers (executive, legislative, judicial)
4. Protection of fundamental human rights
5. Free and fair elections
6. Independent judiciary

**Arms of government in Nigeria** (Federal Republic, Presidential system):
| Arm | Role | Institution |
|-----|------|------------|
| Executive | Implements laws; runs the government | President, Vice President, Ministers, Governors |
| Legislature | Makes laws | National Assembly (Senate + House of Representatives); State Houses of Assembly |
| Judiciary | Interprets laws; administers justice | Supreme Court, Court of Appeal, Federal/State High Courts |

**Checks and balances**: each arm limits the power of the other two to prevent abuse

**Citizenship**: being a legally recognised member of a state, with rights and responsibilities

**Rights of a citizen**:
- Right to life, dignity, personal liberty, fair hearing
- Right to freedom of expression, movement, association
- Right to education, work, and social security (economic rights)

**Responsibilities of a citizen**:
- Obeying laws
- Paying taxes
- Voting in elections
- Serving in the military (where required)
- Protecting public property
- Being honest and hardworking

## 2. SAMPLE PROBLEMS (5 Increasing Difficulty)

### Problem 1 (EASY — JSS1)
**Q**: Which type of family consists of a husband, wife and their children?

**Answer**: **Nuclear family**

---

### Problem 2 (EASY — JSS2)
**Q**: List THREE positive cultural practices in Nigeria.

**Sample Answer**:
1. Respect for elders
2. Communal labour (e.g., *Àárò* in Yoruba culture)
3. Hospitality to visitors

---

### Problem 3 (MEDIUM — JSS2)
**Q**: State TWO causes and TWO effects of unemployment in Nigeria.

**Answer**:
**Causes**: (1) Rapid population growth outpacing job creation; (2) Mismatch between educational qualifications and labour market needs

**Effects**: (1) Increased poverty; (2) Rise in crime rate

---

### Problem 4 (MEDIUM — JSS3)
**Q**: Name and describe the three arms of government in Nigeria.

**Answer**:
1. **Executive** — implements laws and manages the day-to-day administration of the country (led by the President at federal level)
2. **Legislature** — makes laws for the country (National Assembly: Senate + House of Representatives)
3. **Judiciary** — interprets laws and administers justice (Supreme Court, Court of Appeal, etc.)

---

### Problem 5 (HARD — JSS3)
**Q**: "Democracy is the best system of government." Discuss this statement with reference to its principles and limitations.

**Sample Answer (structured)**:
- **Supporting the statement**: Democracy protects human rights, ensures peaceful transfer of power, promotes accountability and popular participation through elections.
- **Limitations / opposing view**: Democracy can be slow (debate takes time); it requires an educated electorate; it can be manipulated by money and propaganda; it may not work well in societies with deep tribal/ethnic divisions.
- **Conclusion**: Despite its limitations, democracy remains the most humane and just form of government because it places power with the people.

## 3. COMMON MISTAKES IN SOCIAL STUDIES

| Mistake | Correction |
|---------|-----------|
| Confusing nuclear and extended family | Nuclear = parents + children; Extended = all relatives |
| Saying "all cultural practices are good" | Distinguish between positive and harmful practices |
| Confusing arms and tiers of government | Arms = Executive/Legislature/Judiciary; Tiers = Federal/State/Local |
| "Democracy = one person decides" | Democracy = rule by the PEOPLE through elected representatives |
| Listing responsibilities when asked for rights (or vice versa) | Rights = what you receive; Responsibilities = what you owe |

## 4. EXAM QUICK TIPS

**Tip 1**: For "discuss" or "examine" questions — present both sides; give your conclusion

**Tip 2**: **Mnemonic for arms of government**: "Every Law Is Judged" (Executive, Legislative, Judiciary)

**Tip 3**: Learn one example for each type of government (democracy = Nigeria/USA; monarchy = Saudi Arabia)

**Tip 4**: When asked for "functions" — list at least 3–5 points with brief explanations

**Tip 5**: For social problems questions — always give causes, effects AND possible solutions
