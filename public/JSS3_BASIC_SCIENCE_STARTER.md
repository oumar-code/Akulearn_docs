## 1. KEY CONCEPTS (Master These)

### Concept 1: Genetics and Heredity (Introduction)

**Key Terms**:
| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| Gene | Unit of heredity; a segment of DNA that codes for a trait |
| Chromosome | Thread-like structure in cell nucleus that carries genes |
| Allele | Alternative forms of a gene (e.g., tall/short) |
| Dominant allele | The allele that is expressed when present (e.g., T for tall) |
| Recessive allele | Only expressed when two copies are present (e.g., t for short) |
| Genotype | Genetic make-up (e.g., TT, Tt, tt) |
| Phenotype | Observable trait / physical appearance (e.g., tall, short) |
| Homozygous | Two identical alleles (TT or tt) |
| Heterozygous | Two different alleles (Tt) |

**Punnet Square** — predict offspring ratios:
```
Cross: Tt × Tt (both parents heterozygous tall)

         T        t
    ┌────────┬────────┐
  T │  TT   │  Tt   │
    ├────────┼────────┤
  t │  Tt   │  tt   │
    └────────┴────────┘

Results: TT : Tt : tt = 1 : 2 : 1
Phenotype ratio: 3 Tall : 1 Short
```

**Sex determination in humans**:
- Females: XX
- Males: XY
- 50% chance of either sex in every pregnancy

### Concept 2: Ecology — Ecosystems and Food Chains

**Key Definitions**:
| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| Ecosystem | All living (biotic) and non-living (abiotic) components in an area |
| Habitat | The specific environment where an organism lives |
| Population | All individuals of one species in an area |
| Community | All populations of different species in the same area |
| Biotic factors | Living parts of the environment (plants, animals, bacteria) |
| Abiotic factors | Non-living parts (temperature, light, water, soil, pH) |
| Producer | Organism that makes its own food (plants via photosynthesis) |
| Consumer | Organism that eats other organisms |
| Decomposer | Breaks down dead organic matter (bacteria, fungi) |

**Food chains and webs**:
```
Grass → Grasshopper → Frog → Snake → Hawk

Rule: Arrow shows direction of ENERGY FLOW
First link = Producer (always a plant)
```

**Trophic levels**:
| Level | Name | Example |
|-------|------|---------|
| 1st | Producer | Grass |
| 2nd | Primary consumer (herbivore) | Grasshopper |
| 3rd | Secondary consumer (carnivore) | Frog |
| 4th | Tertiary consumer | Snake |
| 5th | Apex predator | Hawk |

**Energy loss at each level**: Only ~10% of energy passes to the next level (10% rule).
- Pyramid of energy: widest at base (producers), narrows upward

**Carbon cycle key processes**: Photosynthesis, Respiration, Decomposition, Combustion, Fossilisation

### Concept 3: Carbon and Its Compounds (Organic Chemistry — Intro)

**Why carbon is special**:
1. Forms 4 bonds (tetravalent)
2. Can chain with other carbon atoms → millions of compounds
3. Forms single, double and triple bonds

**Homologous series** (families of organic compounds):
| Series | General formula | Functional group | Example |
|--------|----------------|-----------------|---------|
| Alkanes | CₙH₂ₙ₊₂ | None (all single bonds) | Methane (CH₄), Ethane (C₂H₆) |
| Alkenes | CₙH₂ₙ | C=C double bond | Ethene (C₂H₄) |
| Alkynes | CₙH₂ₙ₋₂ | C≡C triple bond | Ethyne (C₂H₂) |
| Alcohols | CₙH₂ₙ₊₁OH | –OH | Ethanol (C₂H₅OH) |

**Important reactions**:
- Combustion: CH₄ + 2O₂ → CO₂ + 2H₂O (burning, releases energy)
- Complete combustion → CO₂ + H₂O
- Incomplete combustion → CO (carbon monoxide, poisonous) + H₂O

**Petroleum products** (distillation fractions — small → large molecule):
```
Refinery gas → Petrol/Gasoline → Kerosene → Diesel → Fuel oil → Bitumen
(BP increases as molecule gets larger)
```

### Concept 4: Electricity and Circuits

**Key Concepts**:
| Term | Definition | Unit |
|------|-----------|------|
| Current (I) | Flow of electric charge | Ampere (A) |
| Voltage/EMF (V) | Electrical pressure driving current | Volt (V) |
| Resistance (R) | Opposition to current flow | Ohm (Ω) |

**Ohm's Law**: V = IR (Voltage = Current × Resistance)
```
Example: A 12 V battery drives a 3 A current. Find resistance.
R = V/I = 12/3 = 4 Ω
```

**Series circuit**:
- Same current everywhere: I_total = I₁ = I₂
- Total resistance: R_total = R₁ + R₂ + …
- Total voltage divided across components

**Parallel circuit**:
- Total current splits: I_total = I₁ + I₂
- Same voltage across all branches
- Total resistance: 1/R_total = 1/R₁ + 1/R₂ + … (total R < smallest R)

**Electrical power**:
```
P = IV = V²/R = I²R   (P in Watts, W)

Energy = Power × Time = Pt (Joules) or kWh (kilowatt-hours for billing)
```

**Safety devices**:
- **Fuse**: melts and breaks circuit if current is too high — prevents fire
- **Circuit breaker**: automatically switches off; can be reset
- **Earth wire**: directs excess current safely to ground

### Concept 5: Reproduction in Humans (Continuation from JSS2)

**Male reproductive system — key structures**:
| Structure | Function |
|-----------|----------|
| Testes | Produce sperm and testosterone |
| Epididymis | Stores and matures sperm |
| Vas deferens | Carries sperm to urethra |
| Seminal vesicle, prostate gland | Produce seminal fluid to nourish sperm |
| Urethra | Common passage for urine and semen (not at same time) |

**Female reproductive system — key structures**:
| Structure | Function |
|-----------|----------|
| Ovaries | Produce eggs (ova) and hormones (oestrogen, progesterone) |
| Fallopian tubes (oviducts) | Carry egg from ovary to uterus; site of fertilisation |
| Uterus (womb) | Site of implantation and development of foetus |
| Cervix | Lower narrow part of uterus |
| Vagina | Birth canal; receives sperm during intercourse |

**Menstrual cycle** (28-day average):
```
Day 1–5:   Menstruation (lining shed)
Day 6–13:  Follicular phase (egg matures)
Day 14:    Ovulation (egg released)
Day 15–28: Luteal phase (lining thickens; if no fertilisation → cycle repeats)
```

**Fertilisation and development**:
- Fertilisation occurs in the fallopian tube
- Zygote implants in uterine wall → develops over ~9 months (gestation)
- Placenta: exchanges nutrients, oxygen and waste between mother and foetus

## 2. SAMPLE PROBLEMS (5 Increasing Difficulty)

### Problem 1 (EASY — Genetics)
**Q**: A tall plant (TT) is crossed with a short plant (tt). What is the phenotype of all offspring?

**Solution**:
```
TT × tt → all Tt
Tt = heterozygous tall (T is dominant)
```
**Answer**: All offspring are **tall**

---

### Problem 2 (EASY — Electricity)
**Q**: A bulb has resistance 5 Ω and is connected to a 10 V source. Find the current.

**Solution**:
```
I = V/R = 10/5 = 2 A
```
**Answer**: **2 A**

---

### Problem 3 (MEDIUM — Food Chain)
**Q**: Study the food chain: Maize → Caterpillar → Lizard → Snake → Eagle. If DDT (a pesticide) enters the maize, which organism will have the highest concentration of DDT?

**Answer**: **Eagle** (apex predator)

**Explanation**: Toxins **bioaccumulate** — they are stored, not broken down, so concentration increases at each higher trophic level. The eagle at the top gets the highest dose.

---

### Problem 4 (MEDIUM — Circuit)
**Q**: Two resistors of 4 Ω and 12 Ω are connected in parallel. Find the total resistance.

**Solution**:
```
1/R = 1/4 + 1/12 = 3/12 + 1/12 = 4/12 = 1/3
R = 3 Ω
```
**Answer**: **3 Ω** (total R is less than the smallest individual R — correct for parallel)

---

### Problem 5 (HARD — Genetics Punnet Square)
**Q**: Two parents are both carriers of sickle cell anaemia (HbAHbS × HbAHbS). What percentage of offspring will have sickle cell disease (HbSHbS)?

**Solution**:
```
         HbA      HbS
    ┌──────────┬──────────┐
HbA │ HbAHbA  │ HbAHbS  │
    ├──────────┼──────────┤
HbS │ HbAHbS  │ HbSHbS  │
    └──────────┴──────────┘

Results: 1 normal (HbAHbA) : 2 carriers (HbAHbS) : 1 sickle cell disease (HbSHbS)
```
**Answer**: **25%** will have sickle cell disease

## 3. COMMON MISTAKES IN JSS3 BASIC SCIENCE

| Mistake | Correction |
|---------|-----------|
| "Arrows in food chains show who eats what" | Arrows show the direction of **energy flow** |
| TT and tt offspring being "mixed" | TT × tt always gives ALL Tt (all heterozygous) |
| Series and parallel circuit formulas swapped | Series: R_total = sum; Parallel: 1/R = sum of 1/Rs |
| "Complete combustion produces carbon monoxide" | CO is from **incomplete** combustion; complete = CO₂ + H₂O |
| Fertilisation occurs in the uterus | Fertilisation occurs in the **fallopian tube**; implantation in the uterus |

## 5. EXAM QUICK TIPS

**Tip 1**: **Punnet square** — always label parent alleles across top and left side before filling boxes

**Tip 2**: **Energy pyramid** — lowest level is widest (most energy); arrow points upward (energy flows up)

**Tip 3**: **Parallel resistance** — result is always LESS than the smallest resistor — if your answer is larger, recheck

**Tip 4**: **Combustion** — complete = CO₂ + H₂O; incomplete = CO + H₂O (soot possible too)

**Tip 5**: **Fertilisation vs implantation** — these are different events in different locations
