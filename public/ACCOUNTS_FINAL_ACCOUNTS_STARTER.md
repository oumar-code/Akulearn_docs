## 1. KEY CONCEPTS (Master These)

### Concept 1: The Double Entry Principle

**Definition**: Every business transaction affects at least two accounts; for every debit entry, there must be an equal and opposite credit entry

```
Debit (DR)  =  Credit (CR)
```

**The Accounting Equation**:
```
Assets = Capital + Liabilities

Assets       = What the business OWNS (cash, machinery, debtors, stock)
Liabilities  = What the business OWES (creditors, loans, bank overdraft)
Capital      = Owner's investment (Equity = Assets – Liabilities)
```

**Rules of Double Entry**:
| Account Type | Debit (Increase) | Credit (Increase) |
|-------------|-----------------|-----------------|
| Assets (cash, equipment, debtors) | ↑ Debit increases | ↓ Credit decreases |
| Liabilities (creditors, loans) | ↓ Debit decreases | ↑ Credit increases |
| Capital | ↓ Debit decreases | ↑ Credit increases |
| Revenue / Income | ↓ Debit decreases | ↑ Credit increases |
| Expenses | ↑ Debit increases | ↓ Credit decreases |

**Memory Aid – DEAD CLIC**:
```
DEAD = Debit increases: Expenses, Assets, Drawings
CLIC = Credit increases: Liabilities, Income, Capital
```

---

### Concept 2: Ledger Accounts (T-Accounts)

**Ledger**: The book (or system) where all accounts are kept

**Format of a T-Account**:
```
         [Account Name]
─────────────────────────────────────────
   DR (Debit side)    │    CR (Credit side)
─────────────────────────────────────────
Date | Particulars | ₦  │ Date | Particulars | ₦
─────────────────────────────────────────
```

**Balancing an Account**:
1. Add up both sides
2. The larger total goes on both sides
3. The balancing figure is labelled "Balance c/d" (carried down) on the smaller side
4. Bring down the balance as "Balance b/d" (brought down) on the next period

**Types of Ledgers**:
- **Sales Ledger**: Accounts for debtors (customers who owe money)
- **Purchases Ledger**: Accounts for creditors (suppliers owed money)
- **General (Nominal) Ledger**: All other accounts (expenses, capital, assets)
- **Cash Book**: Acts as both a journal and a ledger for cash and bank transactions

---

### Concept 3: Trial Balance

**Definition**: A list of all ledger account balances at a specific date; tests whether total debits equal total credits

**Format**:
```
         TRIAL BALANCE as at [Date]
────────────────────────────────────────────────
Account Name              │  DR (₦)  │  CR (₦)
────────────────────────────────────────────────
Cash                      │  50,000  │
Capital                   │          │  80,000
Purchases                 │  40,000  │
Sales                     │          │  60,000
Salaries                  │  15,000  │
Debtors                   │  20,000  │
Creditors                 │          │  10,000
Equipment                 │  25,000  │
────────────────────────────────────────────────
TOTAL                     │ 150,000  │ 150,000
────────────────────────────────────────────────
```

**Rules for Trial Balance**:
| Item | Side |
|------|------|
| Assets (Cash, Debtors, Equipment, Stock) | Debit |
| Expenses (Wages, Rent, Insurance) | Debit |
| Capital | Credit |
| Liabilities (Creditors, Loans, Bank overdraft) | Credit |
| Revenue/Income (Sales, Rent received) | Credit |

**Limitations of Trial Balance**:
- A balanced TB does NOT mean there are no errors
- Errors NOT detected by TB:
  1. Errors of omission (transaction completely omitted)
  2. Errors of commission (correct amount, wrong account – both accounts of same type)
  3. Errors of principle (correct amount, wrong type of account, e.g., expense debited as asset)
  4. Compensating errors (two errors that cancel each other out)
  5. Original entry errors (same wrong amount entered in both accounts)

### Concept 4: Final Accounts

**Definition**: Financial statements prepared at the end of an accounting period to show profitability and financial position

**Three Final Accounts**:

#### 1. Trading Account
```
PURPOSE: Find Gross Profit

FORMAT:
DR                              TRADING ACCOUNT                      CR
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Opening stock         X     │  Sales                              X
Purchases            X      │  (Less) Returns inward             (X)
Carriage inwards     X      │  ──────────────────────────────────
──────────────────────────  │  Net Sales / Turnover              X
Cost of Goods Available X   │
(Less) Closing stock (X)    │
──────────────────────────  │
Cost of Goods Sold   X      │
Gross Profit (balancing    X│
figure → to P&L)            │
```

**Key Formula**:
```
Gross Profit = Net Sales – Cost of Goods Sold
Cost of Goods Sold = Opening Stock + Purchases – Closing Stock
```

#### 2. Profit and Loss Account (P&L)
```
PURPOSE: Find Net Profit (or Net Loss)

FORMAT:
DR                        PROFIT AND LOSS ACCOUNT                  CR
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Expenses (wages, rent,    │  Gross Profit b/d                    X
insurance, depreciation,  │  Other Income (commission             X
bad debts, etc.)          │  received, rent received)
──────────────────────────│
Net Profit (CR if profit) X│
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
```

**Key Formula**:
```
Net Profit = Gross Profit + Other Income – All Expenses
Net Loss occurs when Total Expenses > Gross Profit + Other Income
```

#### 3. Balance Sheet
```
PURPOSE: Show financial position (Assets vs Capital + Liabilities)
NOT an account – does NOT have DR/CR; shows a snapshot at a date

FORMAT:
         BALANCE SHEET as at [Date]
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
ASSETS                      ₦    CAPITAL AND LIABILITIES      ₦
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
FIXED ASSETS:                    CAPITAL:
  Land & Buildings          X      Opening Capital             X
  Machinery                 X      + Net Profit                X
  Furniture                 X      – Drawings                 (X)
  (Less) Depreciation      (X)     = Closing Capital           X
─────────────────────────────    ─────────────────────────────
Net Fixed Assets            X    LONG-TERM LIABILITIES:
                                   Bank Loan                   X
CURRENT ASSETS:                  ─────────────────────────────
  Stock/Inventory           X    CURRENT LIABILITIES:
  Debtors                   X      Creditors                   X
  Cash at bank              X      Accruals                    X
  Cash in hand              X
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
TOTAL ASSETS                X    TOTAL CAP + LIABILITIES      X
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
```

## 2. SAMPLE PROBLEMS (5 Increasing Difficulty)

### Problem 1 (EASY – Double Entry)
**Q**: A business buys goods on credit for ₦20,000. What is the correct double entry?

**Options**:
A) Debit Purchases; Credit Cash
B) Debit Cash; Credit Purchases
C) Debit Purchases; Credit Creditors ← **CORRECT**
D) Debit Creditors; Credit Purchases

**Explanation**:
- Purchases (expense) → Debit (DEAD rule)
- Bought ON CREDIT → owe the supplier → Creditors (liability) increases → Credit
- If paid cash: Debit Purchases, Credit Cash

**Key Concept**: Credit purchase → Debit Purchases + Credit Creditors

---

### Problem 2 (EASY – Trial Balance Placement)
**Q**: In a Trial Balance, the Capital account appears on the:

**Options**:
A) Debit side
B) Credit side ← **CORRECT**
C) Either side depending on the balance
D) It is not included in the Trial Balance

**Explanation**:
- Capital = owner's equity → a credit balance (CLIC rule)
- Assets and expenses → Debit
- Capital, income, and liabilities → Credit

**Key Concept**: CLIC → Credit increases Capital, Liabilities, Income, Capital

---

### Problem 3 (MEDIUM – Gross Profit)
**Q**: Opening stock = ₦10,000; Purchases = ₦50,000; Closing stock = ₦15,000; Sales = ₦80,000. Calculate Gross Profit.

**Options**:
A) ₦30,000
B) ₦35,000 ← **CORRECT**
C) ₦45,000
D) ₦25,000

**Solution**:
```
Cost of Goods Sold = Opening Stock + Purchases – Closing Stock
                   = 10,000 + 50,000 – 15,000
                   = 45,000

Gross Profit = Sales – Cost of Goods Sold
             = 80,000 – 45,000
             = ₦35,000
```

**Key Concept**: COGS = Opening Stock + Purchases – Closing Stock; GP = Sales – COGS

---

### Problem 4 (MEDIUM – Net Profit)
**Q**: Gross Profit = ₦40,000; Rent = ₦5,000; Wages = ₦12,000; Rent received = ₦3,000. Calculate Net Profit.

**Options**:
A) ₦26,000 ← **CORRECT**
B) ₦23,000
C) ₦29,000
D) ₦17,000

**Solution**:
```
Net Profit = Gross Profit + Other Income – Expenses
           = 40,000 + 3,000 – (5,000 + 12,000)
           = 43,000 – 17,000
           = ₦26,000
```

**Key Concept**: Add other income to gross profit; subtract ALL expenses to get net profit

---

### Problem 5 (HARD – Balance Sheet Classification)
**Q**: Which of the following would appear as a CURRENT LIABILITY on a balance sheet?

**Options**:
A) Land and Buildings
B) Capital
C) Debtors
D) Creditors ← **CORRECT**

**Explanation**:
- Current Liabilities = short-term debts, payable within one year
- Creditors = money owed to suppliers → current liability
- Debtors = money owed TO the business → current asset
- Land and Buildings = fixed asset; Capital = equity (not a liability)

**Key Concept**: Creditors = current liability; Debtors = current asset

## 3. COMMON MISTAKES IN ACCOUNTS

| Mistake | Why Wrong | Correct Understanding |
|---------|-----------|------------------------|
| Putting Capital on the Debit side of TB | Capital is not an asset | Capital is equity → Credit in TB |
| Confusing Debtors and Creditors | Both relate to money | Debtors OWE us (asset); Creditors are OWED by us (liability) |
| Forgetting to deduct Closing Stock in COGS | Overstates cost | COGS = Opening + Purchases – Closing Stock |
| Treating Drawings as an expense (P&L) | Drawings reduce capital, not profit | Drawings go on the Balance Sheet (reduces Capital) |
| Saying a balanced Trial Balance means no errors | TB can still have hidden errors | 5 types of errors are not detected by TB |

## 4. KEY DIAGRAMS (To Understand)

### Diagram 1: Double Entry Summary
```
ASSET increases   → Debit
ASSET decreases   → Credit

EXPENSE increases → Debit
INCOME increases  → Credit

LIABILITY increases → Credit
CAPITAL increases   → Credit

Memory: DEAD CLIC
  DEAD = Debit: Expenses, Assets, Drawings
  CLIC = Credit: Liabilities, Income, Capital
```

### Diagram 2: Final Accounts Flow
```
Trading Account → Gross Profit ──→ P&L Account → Net Profit
                                                      │
                                                      ↓
                                                Balance Sheet
                                           (Net Profit added to Capital)
```

### Diagram 3: Balance Sheet Structure
```
LEFT SIDE                    RIGHT SIDE
(What we OWN = Assets)       (How we FUNDED it)
─────────────────────────────────────────────────
Fixed Assets                 Capital (+ Net Profit – Drawings)
  └── Minus Depreciation
Current Assets               Long-term Liabilities
  Stock                      Current Liabilities
  Debtors                      Creditors
  Cash                         Overdraft
─────────────────────────────────────────────────
TOTAL ASSETS = CAPITAL + LIABILITIES
```

## 5. EXAM QUICK TIPS

**Tip 1**: **DEAD CLIC – the most important memory aid in Accounting**
- Debit: Expenses, Assets, Drawings
- Credit: Liabilities, Income, Capital

**Tip 2**: **Gross Profit formula: GP = Sales – COGS; COGS = Op. Stock + Purchases – Cl. Stock**
- Always deduct closing stock; it's not yet "used up"

**Tip 3**: **Net Profit: start with GP, ADD other income, SUBTRACT all expenses**
- Drawings are NOT expenses (they go to the Balance Sheet)

**Tip 4**: **Debtors are ASSETS (owe us); Creditors are LIABILITIES (we owe them)**
- This distinction appears in both Trial Balance and Balance Sheet questions

**Tip 5**: **Balance Sheet MUST always balance: Assets = Capital + Liabilities**
- If it doesn't balance, you've made an error

## 8. REAL WAEC/JAMB ACCOUNTS PATTERNS

**Pattern 1**: "The double entry for [transaction] is…"
- Apply DEAD CLIC rule; identify what increases and decreases

**Pattern 2**: "From the following information, calculate Gross Profit / Net Profit"
- Apply COGS formula then GP formula then NP formula in sequence

**Pattern 3**: "[Item] would appear on which side of the Trial Balance?"
- Asset/Expense → Debit; Liability/Income/Capital → Credit

**Pattern 4**: "Which of the following errors would NOT be revealed by a Trial Balance?"
- Any of the 5 undetectable errors: Omission, Commission, Principle, Compensating, Original entry

**Pattern 5**: "[Item] would appear under which section of the Balance Sheet?"
- Fixed asset, Current asset, Capital, Current liability, or Long-term liability
