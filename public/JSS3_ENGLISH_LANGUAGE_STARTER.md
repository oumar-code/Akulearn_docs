## 1. KEY CONCEPTS (Master These)

### Concept 1: Advanced Essay Writing for BECE

**BECE Essay Paper structure**:
- Write ONE composition from given topics (narrative, descriptive, or argumentative)
- Typically 200–250 words
- Marked on: Content/Relevance (40%), Organisation (20%), Expression (20%), Grammar (20%)

**Essay Types and Approaches**:

| Type | Introduction | Body | Conclusion |
|------|-------------|------|-----------|
| Narrative | Hook + introduce setting/character | Events in time order; build to climax | Resolution; what was learnt |
| Descriptive | Create atmosphere; state what is described | Describe in sections (appearance, atmosphere, details) | Overall impression/feeling |
| Argumentative | State your position clearly | 3 reasons with evidence; address counter-argument | Restate position; call to action |
| Informal letter | Address + date + salutation | Introduction, 2–3 paragraphs of content, conclusion | Closing remark + sign-off |
| Formal letter | Both addresses + date + salutation | Subject line + formal paragraphs | Correct close + signature |

**Narrative techniques to impress examiners**:
- Vary sentence length (short for impact; long for description)
- Use dialogue: "Stop!" he cried. "The bridge is broken!"
- Use sensory language: what you see, hear, smell, feel
- Show, don't tell: "His hands trembled" not "He was nervous"
- Use time connectors: First, Meanwhile, Eventually, At last, By the time

### Concept 2: Complex Sentences and Subordinate Clauses

**Types of Clauses**:
| Clause Type | Definition | Example |
|-------------|-----------|---------|
| Main clause | Can stand alone | "She studied hard." |
| Subordinate (dependent) clause | Cannot stand alone; depends on main clause | "…because she wanted to pass." |

**Subordinating conjunctions** (introduce subordinate clauses):
```
Time:      when, while, before, after, as soon as, until, once
Cause:     because, since, as
Contrast:  although, though, even though, whereas, while
Condition: if, unless, provided that, as long as
Purpose:   so that, in order that
```

**Relative clauses**:
- Defining (no commas): "The student **who studied** passed." (essential information)
- Non-defining (with commas): "Emeka, **who studies every day**, is top of the class." (extra info)

**Sentence types** (for BECE grammar questions):
| Type | Structure | Example |
|------|-----------|---------|
| Simple | 1 main clause | "The boy ran." |
| Compound | 2+ main clauses (and/but/or/so) | "She read, and she slept." |
| Complex | 1 main + 1+ subordinate | "He left before she arrived." |
| Compound-complex | 2 main + 1+ subordinate | "She studied and she succeeded because she worked hard." |

### Concept 3: Reading Comprehension — Advanced Strategies

**PQRST Method**:
```
P — Preview: read title, headings, first and last sentences
Q — Question: convert headings/topic into questions before reading
R — Read: read actively, underlining key points
S — Summarise: after each paragraph, recall the main point
T — Test: answer questions without looking; then check
```

**Types of comprehension questions and strategies**:
| Question type | Strategy |
|---------------|---------|
| Literal/factual | Locate exact words in passage |
| Inference | Use "reading between the lines"; clues in text |
| Vocabulary in context | Use surrounding words to determine meaning; test each option |
| Summary | Find the main point (usually topic sentence of each paragraph) |
| Writer's purpose/tone | Identify the writer's attitude: informing? persuading? entertaining? |
| Titling | Identify the central/unifying theme of the whole passage |

**Tone words** (to describe a writer's attitude):
Angry, critical, humorous, ironic, sarcastic, optimistic, pessimistic, formal, informal, nostalgic, persuasive, objective, subjective

### Concept 4: Grammar — Common Errors (JSS3 Level)

**Concord (Subject–Verb Agreement) — complex cases**:
| Rule | Example |
|------|---------|
| Collective nouns take singular verb | "The committee **has** decided." |
| "Each / every / either / neither" → singular | "Each of the girls **is** present." |
| Subjects joined by "or/nor" → verb agrees with nearer subject | "Neither she nor her brothers **are** coming." |
| Indefinite pronouns "everyone, nobody, someone" → singular | "Everyone **has** a role to play." |
| "The number of …" → singular | "The number of students **is** large." |
| "A number of …" → plural | "A number of students **are** absent." |

**Conditional sentences**:
| Type | Form | Use |
|------|------|-----|
| Zero conditional | If + present, present | General truths: "If you heat water, it boils." |
| First conditional | If + present, will + base | Possible future: "If she studies, she will pass." |
| Second conditional | If + past, would + base | Unlikely/imaginary: "If I were rich, I would travel." |
| Third conditional | If + past perfect, would have + PP | Impossible past: "If he had studied, he would have passed." |

**Phrasal verbs** (common in BECE):
| Phrasal Verb | Meaning |
|-------------|---------|
| look after | take care of |
| look forward to | anticipate with pleasure |
| give up | stop / surrender |
| turn down | refuse / reduce volume |
| run out of | exhaust a supply |
| put off | postpone |
| break down | stop working / collapse |

### Concept 5: BECE Oral English — Stress and Intonation

**Word Stress**: every word of two or more syllables has one syllable that is stressed (said more strongly).
- STU-dent (stress on first)
- be-HIND (stress on second)
- un-der-STAND (stress on third)

**Sentence Stress**: content words (nouns, main verbs, adjectives, adverbs) are stressed; function words (articles, prepositions, conjunctions) are usually unstressed.
```
"SHE SENT a LETTER to her FRIEND." (stressed = capital)
```

**Intonation**:
- **Falling intonation** ↘ — statements, commands, wh-questions
- **Rising intonation** ↗ — yes/no questions, polite requests, unfinished lists
- **Fall-Rise** ↘↗ — hesitation, implication, partial agreement

**Common pronunciation pairs to distinguish** (for oral BECE):
| Pair | /iː/ vs /ɪ/ | /æ/ vs /e/ |
|------|-------------|------------|
| | seat / sit | bat / bet |
| | sheep / ship | man / men |
| | heat / hit | cap / kept |

## 2. SAMPLE PROBLEMS (5 Increasing Difficulty)

### Problem 1 (EASY — Subject–Verb Agreement)
**Q**: Choose the correct verb: "Each of the students ___ a role to play." (have / has)

**Answer**: **has**

**Explanation**: "Each" is singular — always takes a singular verb even when followed by a plural noun.

---

### Problem 2 (EASY — Sentence Type)
**Q**: Identify the sentence type: "Although she was tired, she continued studying."

**Answer**: **Complex sentence** (one main clause + one subordinate clause introduced by "Although")

---

### Problem 3 (MEDIUM — Conditional)
**Q**: Complete using the correct conditional: "If he ___ (study) harder, he ___ (pass) the BECE last year."

**Answer**: "If he **had studied** harder, he **would have passed** the BECE last year."

**Explanation**: This refers to an impossible past situation → **Third conditional** (if + past perfect, would have + past participle).

---

### Problem 4 (MEDIUM — Comprehension, Tone)
**Q**: A passage opens: "It is nothing short of criminal negligence that half the schools in this country still lack basic sanitation."

"What is the writer's tone?"

**Answer**: **Angry / Critical / Indignant**

**Explanation**: Strong language ("criminal negligence") expresses strong disapproval — this is not objective or informative; it is emotionally charged and critical.

---

### Problem 5 (HARD — Essay Opening)
**Q**: Write the opening paragraph of a narrative essay titled "A Day I Will Never Forget."

**Sample Answer**: 
"The morning of 14 March had begun like any other — the familiar crow of roosters, the smell of mama's groundnut soup drifting from the kitchen, the gentle cool of the Harmattan wind against my cheek. I had no reason to suspect that before sunset, my world would change forever."

**Why it works**:
- Sensory details (smell, sound, touch)
- Suspense created in the final sentence
- Establishes time and setting immediately
- Varied sentence structure

## 3. COMMON MISTAKES IN JSS3 ENGLISH

| Mistake | Correction |
|---------|-----------|
| "Each of the boys have …" | "Each of the boys **has** …" (each = singular) |
| "If I was rich, I would …" | "If I **were** rich, I would …" (second conditional uses past subjunctive) |
| "They told me to gave him …" | "They told me to **give** him …" (infinitive after "to") |
| Not varying sentence structure in essays | Mix simple, compound and complex sentences |
| Using only "said" in dialogue | Use "whispered", "cried", "replied", "added", "warned" for variety |

## 5. EXAM QUICK TIPS

**Tip 1**: **Essay** — plan for 3 minutes; write for 22 minutes; review for 5 minutes

**Tip 2**: **Each / everyone / nobody** — always singular verb; never be misled by a plural noun after "of"

**Tip 3**: **Conditional tense check** — identify whether the situation is real (1st), imaginary present (2nd), or impossible past (3rd)

**Tip 4**: **Comprehension vocabulary** — use context to eliminate wrong options; substitute each option into the sentence

**Tip 5**: **Word stress in oral English** — stress shifts when noun becomes verb: REcord (noun) vs reCORD (verb); PROtest vs proTEST
