## 1. KEY CONCEPTS (Master These)

### Concept 1: Pronouns — Types and Usage

**Types of Pronouns**:
| Type | Examples | Use |
|------|----------|-----|
| Personal | I, you, he, she, it, we, they | Replace nouns referring to people/things |
| Possessive | mine, yours, his, hers, ours, theirs | Show ownership |
| Reflexive | myself, yourself, himself, herself, ourselves | Subject and object are same |
| Demonstrative | this, that, these, those | Point to specific nouns |
| Interrogative | who, whom, which, what | Ask questions |
| Relative | who, whom, whose, which, that | Introduce relative clauses |
| Indefinite | anyone, everyone, nobody, each, all, some | Refer non-specifically |

**Subject vs Object Pronouns**:
| Subject (does the action) | Object (receives the action) |
|--------------------------|------------------------------|
| I | me |
| he | him |
| she | her |
| we | us |
| they | them |

**Common errors**:
- ✗ "Him and I went to school." → ✅ "He and I went to school."
- ✗ "She gave the book to John and I." → ✅ "She gave the book to John and **me**."

### Concept 2: Adjectives and Degrees of Comparison

**Degrees of Comparison**:
| Degree | Short adjective | Long adjective |
|--------|----------------|----------------|
| Positive | tall | intelligent |
| Comparative | taller (than) | more intelligent (than) |
| Superlative | tallest | most intelligent |

**Rules**:
- One syllable → add -er / -est: tall → taller → tallest
- Ends in -e → add -r / -st: large → larger → largest
- Ends consonant + vowel + consonant → double final consonant: big → bigger → biggest
- Two+ syllables → use "more" / "most": beautiful → more beautiful → most beautiful

**Irregular comparisons** (must memorise):
| Positive | Comparative | Superlative |
|---------|------------|------------|
| good | better | best |
| bad | worse | worst |
| little | less | least |
| many/much | more | most |
| far | farther/further | farthest/furthest |

### Concept 3: Paragraph Writing and Essay Types

**Structure of a Good Paragraph (TEEL)**:
```
T — Topic sentence   : States the main idea of the paragraph
E — Explanation      : Explains or develops the main idea
E — Evidence/Example : Gives a specific example or evidence
L — Link             : Links back to the topic or to the next paragraph
```

**Types of Essays (for BECE)**:

| Type | Purpose | Key Features |
|------|---------|-------------|
| Narrative | Tells a story | Characters, setting, plot, climax |
| Descriptive | Describes a person/place/thing | Vivid details, sensory language |
| Argumentative / Discursive | Presents a viewpoint | Reasons, evidence, counter-argument |
| Expository / Informative | Explains or informs | Facts, clear structure, no opinion |

**Essay Planning (Before Writing)**:
1. Read the question carefully — identify the essay type and topic
2. Spend 2–3 minutes brainstorming points
3. Create a brief outline (introduction → 3 body paragraphs → conclusion)
4. Write, then leave 2–3 minutes to check for errors

**Word count guidance for JSS**: 120–180 words in composition exercises

### Concept 4: Direct and Indirect Speech

**Direct Speech**: Reports the exact words spoken, in quotation marks
- She said, **"I am going to the market."**

**Indirect (Reported) Speech**: Reports what was said without exact words, no quotation marks

**Rules when converting to indirect speech**:
| Direct | Change in Indirect |
|--------|-------------------|
| Present → | Past |
| "am/is/are" → | was/were |
| "will" → | would |
| "can" → | could |
| "today" → | that day |
| "tomorrow" → | the next day / the following day |
| "yesterday" → | the previous day |
| "here" → | there |
| "this/these" → | that/those |

**Example**:
- Direct: He said, "I **will** come **tomorrow**."
- Indirect: He said (that) he **would** come **the next day**.

**Say vs Tell**:
- say: does not need an object → "She said she was tired."
- tell: needs an object → "She told **me** she was tired."

## 2. SAMPLE PROBLEMS (5 Increasing Difficulty)

### Problem 1 (EASY — Pronoun)
**Q**: Choose the correct pronoun: "The teacher gave the prize to James and ___." (I / me)

**Answer**: **me**

**Explanation**: "me" is an object pronoun (receives the action of "gave"). Test: remove "James and" → "The teacher gave the prize to **me**."

---

### Problem 2 (EASY — Comparison)
**Q**: Fill in the blank: "Emeka is the ___ student in the class." (tall)

**Answer**: **tallest**

**Explanation**: Superlative degree (comparing more than two). "tall" → tallest (one syllable, no doubling needed).

---

### Problem 3 (MEDIUM — Indirect Speech)
**Q**: Change to indirect speech: She said, "I **am** feeling sick **today**."

**Answer**: She said (that) she **was** feeling sick **that day**.

**Explanation**: "am" → "was"; "today" → "that day". No quotation marks in indirect speech.

---

### Problem 4 (MEDIUM — Essay Structure)
**Q**: Write the topic sentence for a paragraph about the importance of reading.

**Sample Answer**: "Reading regularly broadens a student's vocabulary, deepens their understanding of the world, and strengthens critical thinking skills."

**Explanation**: A topic sentence must state the main idea clearly and be broad enough to cover the paragraph's content.

---

### Problem 5 (HARD — Pronoun Case)
**Q**: Identify and correct all errors: "Him and me went to the market. The teacher told John and I to sit down."

**Answer**:
- "Him and me" → **He and I** (both are subjects doing the action)
- "John and I" → **John and me** (object of "told")

## 3. COMMON MISTAKES IN JSS2 ENGLISH

| Mistake | Correction |
|---------|-----------|
| "Me and John went …" | "John and I went …" (subject pronoun; I comes last) |
| "More taller than" | "Taller than" (never use "more" with -er forms) |
| Forgetting tense change in indirect speech | am/is → was; will → would; etc. |
| Using "says that" instead of "said that" | Past reporting verb → "said that" |
| Not organising essay with TEEL | Each paragraph must have topic, explanation, example, link |

## 5. EXAM QUICK TIPS

**Tip 1**: **Pronoun case — drop other names and test the pronoun alone** ("to John and I" → "to I" ✗ → use "me")

**Tip 2**: **Comparison errors** — never use "more" + "-er" together

**Tip 3**: **Indirect speech** — check all time and place words (today, here, this) and change them

**Tip 4**: **Essay writing** — plan before writing; one idea per paragraph

**Tip 5**: **Say vs tell**: tell ALWAYS needs a person ("told me/him/her/us")
