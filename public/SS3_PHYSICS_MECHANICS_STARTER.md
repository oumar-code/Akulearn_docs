## 1. KEY CONCEPTS (Master These)

### Concept 1: Equations of Motion (Kinematics)

**The 5 Key Variables**:
```
s = displacement (m)
u = initial velocity (m/s)
v = final velocity (m/s)
a = acceleration (m/s²)
t = time (s)
```

**The 4 Equations of Motion** (Must Memorize ALL 4):
```
Equation 1: v = u + at
Equation 2: s = ut + ½at²
Equation 3: v² = u² + 2as
Equation 4: s = ½(u + v)t
```

**When to use which**:
- Missing v → use Equation 2 or 4
- Missing t → use Equation 3
- Missing s → use Equation 1 alone

**Free Fall**: When object falls under gravity with no air resistance
- a = g = 10 m/s² (downward) or 9.8 m/s²
- Use same equations; substitute a = g

**Why Important**: 7-10 JAMB questions involve mechanics calculations every year

---

### Concept 2: Newton's Laws of Motion

**Newton's First Law (Law of Inertia)**:
> A body remains at rest or in uniform motion unless acted upon by an external unbalanced force

- Inertia = resistance to change in motion
- Heavier objects have more inertia

**Newton's Second Law (Law of Acceleration)**:
> Force = mass × acceleration
```
F = ma

Where:
F = force (Newtons, N)
m = mass (kg)
a = acceleration (m/s²)
```

**Newton's Third Law (Action and Reaction)**:
> For every action, there is an equal and opposite reaction

- Force pairs act on DIFFERENT objects
- Example: Gun recoils when bullet is fired

---

### Concept 3: Forces – Weight, Friction, Normal Force

**Weight vs Mass**:
```
Weight (W) = mg

W = weight (Newtons, N)
m = mass (kg)
g = gravitational field strength = 10 N/kg (or 9.8 N/kg)

Note: Mass is the SAME everywhere; Weight changes with gravity
```

**Friction**:
- Force that opposes motion between surfaces
- Friction = μ × Normal Force (F = μN)
- μ = coefficient of friction
- Reduces kinetic energy; causes heat

**Momentum**:
```
p = mv

p = momentum (kg·m/s)
m = mass (kg)
v = velocity (m/s)

Law of Conservation of Momentum:
Total momentum before = Total momentum after (in a closed system)
```

---

### Concept 4: Work, Energy, and Power

**Work Done**:
```
W = F × d × cos θ

W = work done (Joules, J)
F = force applied (N)
d = displacement (m)
θ = angle between force and displacement

If force is parallel to displacement: W = Fd (cos 0° = 1)
```

**Kinetic Energy (KE)**:
```
KE = ½mv²

KE = kinetic energy (J)
m = mass (kg)
v = speed (m/s)
```

**Potential Energy (PE)**:
```
PE = mgh

PE = gravitational potential energy (J)
m = mass (kg)
g = 10 m/s² (or 9.8 m/s²)
h = height above reference point (m)
```

**Conservation of Energy**:
```
At any point: Total Energy = KE + PE = constant (no friction)
At top: KE = 0, PE = maximum
At bottom: KE = maximum, PE = 0
```

**Power**:
```
P = W/t    or    P = Fv

P = power (Watts, W)
W = work done (J)
t = time (s)
v = velocity (m/s)
```

## 2. SAMPLE PROBLEMS (5 Increasing Difficulty)

### Problem 1 (EASY – Newton's Second Law)
**Q**: A net force of 20 N acts on a body of mass 4 kg. What is the acceleration of the body?

**Options**:
A) 80 m/s²
B) 0.2 m/s²
C) 5 m/s² ← **CORRECT**
D) 16 m/s²

**Solution**:
```
F = ma
20 = 4 × a
a = 20/4 = 5 m/s²
```

**Key Concept**: F = ma; isolate a by dividing F by m

---

### Problem 2 (EASY – Equations of Motion)
**Q**: A car starts from rest and accelerates at 3 m/s² for 5 seconds. What is its final velocity?

**Options**:
A) 15 m/s ← **CORRECT**
B) 10 m/s
C) 7.5 m/s
D) 30 m/s

**Solution**:
```
u = 0 (starts from rest)
a = 3 m/s²
t = 5 s
v = ?

Use: v = u + at
v = 0 + (3)(5)
v = 15 m/s
```

**Key Concept**: "Starts from rest" means u = 0

---

### Problem 3 (MEDIUM – Free Fall)
**Q**: A ball is dropped from a height of 45 m. How long does it take to reach the ground? (g = 10 m/s²)

**Options**:
A) 4.5 s
B) 9 s
C) 3 s ← **CORRECT**
D) 6 s

**Solution**:
```
u = 0 (dropped from rest)
a = g = 10 m/s²
s = 45 m
t = ?

Use: s = ut + ½at²
45 = 0(t) + ½(10)(t²)
45 = 5t²
t² = 9
t = 3 s
```

**Key Concept**: For free fall: u = 0, a = g (downward = positive)

---

### Problem 4 (MEDIUM – Energy)
**Q**: A ball of mass 2 kg moves at 6 m/s. What is its kinetic energy?

**Options**:
A) 12 J
B) 36 J ← **CORRECT**
C) 24 J
D) 72 J

**Solution**:
```
KE = ½mv²
KE = ½ × 2 × (6)²
KE = ½ × 2 × 36
KE = 36 J
```

**Key Concept**: KE = ½mv² → square the velocity first!

---

### Problem 5 (HARD – Momentum & Collision)
**Q**: A 3 kg trolley moving at 4 m/s collides with and sticks to a 1 kg trolley at rest. What is their combined velocity after collision?

**Options**:
A) 4 m/s
B) 3 m/s ← **CORRECT**
C) 12 m/s
D) 1 m/s

**Solution**:
```
Conservation of momentum: p_before = p_after

Before:
p₁ = m₁v₁ = 3 × 4 = 12 kg·m/s
p₂ = m₂v₂ = 1 × 0 = 0 kg·m/s
Total p = 12 + 0 = 12 kg·m/s

After (they stick together):
Total mass = 3 + 1 = 4 kg
12 = 4 × v
v = 3 m/s
```

**Key Concept**: In a perfectly inelastic collision, total momentum is conserved

## 3. COMMON MISTAKES IN MECHANICS

| Mistake | Why Wrong | Correct Understanding |
|---------|-----------|------------------------|
| Confusing mass and weight | "My mass is 70 N" | Mass in kg; weight = mg in Newtons |
| Using wrong equation of motion | Missing a needed variable | Check what you're given and what you need |
| Forgetting to square v in KE | KE = ½mv not ½mv² | KE = ½mv² (velocity is squared!) |
| Not using u = 0 for "starts from rest" | Getting wrong answer | "Starts from rest" or "dropped" → u = 0 |
| Missing cos θ in work calculation | W = Fd instead of W = Fd cos θ | Include angle if force is not parallel to motion |

## 5. EXAM QUICK TIPS

**Tip 1**: **Identify given and required variables** before choosing an equation
- List: u, v, a, s, t (what you have vs what you need)
- Pick the equation that has all 4 of those

**Tip 2**: **Free fall shortcuts**
- "Dropped from rest": u = 0, a = g = 10 m/s² (downward)
- "Thrown upward": v = 0 at highest point, a = –g

**Tip 3**: **F = ma is the most tested formula**
- Rearrange as needed: a = F/m or m = F/a
- Units: Force in N, mass in kg, acceleration in m/s²

**Tip 4**: **KE formula: velocity is squared**
- KE = ½mv²
- If velocity doubles: KE increases by a factor of 4 (not 2)

**Tip 5**: **Momentum conservation in collisions**
- "Sticks together" = perfectly inelastic → use m₁u₁ + m₂u₂ = (m₁+m₂)v
- Object at rest: u₂ = 0
