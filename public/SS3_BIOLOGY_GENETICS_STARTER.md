## 1. KEY CONCEPTS (Master These)

### Concept 1: Key Terms in Genetics

**Must-Know Definitions**:

| Term | Definition | Example |
|------|-----------|---------|
| Gene | Unit of heredity controlling a trait | Gene for height |
| Allele | Alternative form of a gene | Tall (T) or short (t) |
| Dominant | Allele that masks the other | T (tall) dominates t |
| Recessive | Allele hidden when paired with dominant | t is recessive |
| Genotype | Genetic make-up of an organism | TT, Tt, tt |
| Phenotype | Observable characteristic | Tall or short |
| Homozygous | Both alleles are the same | TT or tt |
| Heterozygous | Alleles are different | Tt |
| Locus | Position of a gene on a chromosome | Fixed position |

**Exam Frequency**: Term definitions appear in 30-40% of genetics questions

---

### Concept 2: Mendel's Laws

**Mendel's First Law – Law of Segregation**:
> During gamete formation, each pair of alleles separates, and each gamete receives only one allele from each pair

**Mendel's Second Law – Law of Independent Assortment**:
> Alleles of different genes assort independently of each other during gamete formation
> (Only applies to genes on different chromosomes)

**How Mendel Worked**:
- Used pea plants (Pisum sativum) with contrasting traits
- Performed monohybrid and dihybrid crosses
- Analysed ratios in F₁ and F₂ generations

---

### Concept 3: Monohybrid Cross (One Trait)

**Definition**: Cross involving one pair of contrasting traits

**Example: Tall (T) × Short (t)**

**Punnett Square for Tt × Tt**:
```
        T       t
   ┌─────────┬─────────┐
T  │   TT    │   Tt    │
   ├─────────┼─────────┤
t  │   Tt    │   tt    │
   └─────────┴─────────┘
```

**Results**:
- Genotype ratio: TT : Tt : tt = 1 : 2 : 1
- Phenotype ratio: Tall : Short = 3 : 1

**The famous 3:1 ratio** appears in EVERY exam!

---

### Concept 4: Dihybrid Cross (Two Traits)

**Definition**: Cross involving two pairs of contrasting traits

**Example**: Tall Yellow (TTYY) × Short Green (ttyy)

**F₁ Generation**: All TtYy (Tall Yellow) – heterozygous

**F₁ × F₁ Cross (TtYy × TtYy)**:
Gametes from each parent: TY, Ty, tY, ty

**4×4 Punnett Square gives 16 combinations**:
- **9** Tall Yellow (T_Y_)
- **3** Tall Green (T_yy)
- **3** Short Yellow (ttY_)
- **1** Short Short (ttyy)

**Dihybrid phenotype ratio: 9:3:3:1**

**Memory Tip**: Dihybrid ratio = 9:3:3:1 = always adds to 16

---

### Concept 5: Co-dominance and Incomplete Dominance

**Incomplete Dominance**:
- Neither allele is completely dominant
- Heterozygote shows intermediate phenotype
- Example: Red (R) × White (W) → Pink (RW)
- Ratio: 1 Red : 2 Pink : 1 White (1:2:1)

**Co-dominance**:
- Both alleles expressed simultaneously
- Example: ABO blood groups
  - Type A (I^A I^A or I^A i)
  - Type B (I^B I^B or I^B i)
  - Type AB (I^A I^B) – both A and B expressed
  - Type O (ii) – recessive

**ABO Blood Group crosses** appear frequently in WAEC!

## 2. SAMPLE PROBLEMS (5 Increasing Difficulty)

### Problem 1 (EASY – Terms)
**Q**: An organism with two identical alleles for a trait (e.g., TT or tt) is described as:

**Options**:
A) Heterozygous
B) Homozygous ← **CORRECT**
C) Hybrid
D) Recessive

**Explanation**:
- Homozygous = both alleles are the SAME (TT or tt)
- Heterozygous = alleles are DIFFERENT (Tt)
- TT = homozygous dominant; tt = homozygous recessive

**Key Concept**: Homo = same; Hetero = different

---

### Problem 2 (EASY – Punnett Square)
**Q**: Two heterozygous tall pea plants (Tt × Tt) are crossed. What fraction of offspring will be short?

**Options**:
A) 0
B) 1/4 ← **CORRECT**
C) 1/2
D) 3/4

**Solution**:
```
      T     t
  ┌──────┬──────┐
T │  TT  │  Tt  │
  ├──────┼──────┤
t │  Tt  │  tt  │
  └──────┴──────┘

Results: TT, Tt, Tt, tt (4 total)
Short (tt) = 1 out of 4 = 1/4
```

**Key Concept**: Monohybrid Tt × Tt → 3:1 phenotype ratio → 1 in 4 are recessive

---

### Problem 3 (MEDIUM – Cross Analysis)
**Q**: In pea plants, Round (R) is dominant over Wrinkled (r). A round plant crossed with a wrinkled plant produces 50% round and 50% wrinkled offspring. What are the genotypes of the parents?

**Options**:
A) RR × rr
B) Rr × Rr
C) Rr × rr ← **CORRECT**
D) RR × Rr

**Solution**:
```
Rr × rr:
      R     r
  ┌──────┬──────┐
r │  Rr  │  rr  │
  ├──────┼──────┤
r │  Rr  │  rr  │
  └──────┴──────┘

Results: Rr (round), rr (wrinkled) = 50% : 50% ✓

Check: RR × rr → all Rr (100% round – not matching)
       Rr × Rr → 75% round : 25% wrinkled – not matching
```

**Key Concept**: 1:1 ratio always means one parent is heterozygous × homozygous recessive

---

### Problem 4 (MEDIUM – Blood Groups)
**Q**: A man with blood group AB marries a woman with blood group O. What are the possible blood groups of their children?

**Options**:
A) A, B, AB, O
B) AB and O only
C) A and B only ← **CORRECT**
D) AB only

**Solution**:
```
Man: I^A I^B (type AB)
Woman: ii (type O)

Gametes: Man gives I^A or I^B; Woman gives i

Cross:
      I^A      I^B
  ┌────────┬────────┐
i │ I^A i  │ I^B i  │
  │ Type A │ Type B │
  └────────┴────────┘

Possible children: Type A (I^A i) or Type B (I^B i)
No child can be AB or O
```

**Key Concept**: AB parent contributes either I^A or I^B, never both to same gamete

---

### Problem 5 (HARD – Dihybrid Cross)
**Q**: In a dihybrid cross between two plants heterozygous for both seed colour (Yellow Y dominant over green y) and seed texture (Round R dominant over wrinkled r), what proportion of offspring will be green and wrinkled (yyRR)?

**Options**:
A) 9/16
B) 1/16 ← **CORRECT**
C) 3/16
D) 1/4

**Solution**:
```
Cross: YyRr × YyRr

For each trait independently:
- Yy × Yy: probability of yy = 1/4
- Rr × Rr: probability of RR = 1/4

But wait – yyRR (green round):
- yy probability = 1/4
- RR probability = 1/4
- Combined: 1/4 × 1/4 = 1/16 ✓

Note: Green wrinkled (yyrr) = 1/4 × 1/4 = 1/16 too (different genotype)
```

**Key Concept**: In dihybrid crosses, multiply probabilities of each independent trait

## 3. COMMON MISTAKES IN GENETICS

| Mistake | Why Wrong | Correct Understanding |
|---------|-----------|------------------------|
| Mixing phenotype and genotype ratios | 3:1 is phenotype; 1:2:1 is genotype | Always state which ratio you mean |
| Writing gametes incorrectly | TT → gametes are T, T (not TT) | Each gamete carries only ONE allele |
| Forgetting heterozygous means carrier | Think Tt = affected by recessive | Tt = carrier (shows dominant trait) |
| Wrong blood group cross | I^A I^B + ii = AB offspring | AB parent CANNOT pass AB to one gamete |
| Dihybrid ratio as 3:1 | Using monohybrid ratio for 2 traits | Dihybrid = 9:3:3:1 (adds to 16) |

## 5. EXAM QUICK TIPS

**Tip 1**: **Always draw a Punnett square** – even for easy crosses; prevents errors

**Tip 2**: **Know the key ratios by heart**:
- Monohybrid Tt × Tt → 3:1 phenotype, 1:2:1 genotype
- Test cross (dominant × recessive) → 1:1 phenotype
- Dihybrid → 9:3:3:1 phenotype

**Tip 3**: **Blood groups are frequent**
- AB × O → A and B children only
- AB × AB → A, AB, B (no O)
- O × O → all O

**Tip 4**: **1:1 ratio always = test cross**
- One parent is heterozygous (Tt)
- Other parent is homozygous recessive (tt)

**Tip 5**: **Dihybrid tip**
- Total offspring in dihybrid = 16
- Verify: 9+3+3+1 = 16 ✓
