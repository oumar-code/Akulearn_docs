## 1. KEY CONCEPTS (Master These)

### Concept 1: Parts of Speech (The 8 Building Blocks)

**Definition**: Categories that classify every word in English by its function in a sentence

| Part of Speech | Definition | Examples |
|---------------|-----------|---------|
| **Noun** | Name of a person, place, thing, or idea | Ayo, Lagos, book, happiness |
| **Pronoun** | Replaces a noun | he, she, it, they, who, which |
| **Verb** | Shows action or state of being | run, is, write, become |
| **Adjective** | Describes a noun | tall, beautiful, three |
| **Adverb** | Modifies a verb, adjective, or adverb | quickly, very, yesterday |
| **Preposition** | Shows relationship between words | in, on, at, by, for, with |
| **Conjunction** | Joins words or clauses | and, but, or, because, although |
| **Interjection** | Expresses emotion | Oh!, Wow!, Alas! |

**Types of Nouns**:
- Common: dog, city, book
- Proper: Femi, Abuja, Nigeria *(capitalised)*
- Abstract: love, freedom, honesty
- Collective: herd, flock, team, crowd
- Countable: pen (pens); Uncountable: water, rice *(no plural)*

**Types of Pronouns** (Exam-Tested):
| Subject | Object | Possessive (Adj) | Possessive (Pron) | Reflexive |
|---------|--------|-----------------|-------------------|-----------|
| I | me | my | mine | myself |
| he | him | his | his | himself |
| she | her | her | hers | herself |
| they | them | their | theirs | themselves |

### Concept 2: Tenses (12 Tenses Simplified)

**Definition**: Tense shows WHEN an action happens

**The 4 Time Frames × 3 Aspects = 12 Tenses**:

| Tense | Structure | Example |
|-------|-----------|---------|
| Simple Present | V₁ / V₁s | She *writes* daily. |
| Present Continuous | am/is/are + V-ing | He *is writing* now. |
| Present Perfect | has/have + V₃ | I *have written* the letter. |
| Present Perfect Continuous | has/have been + V-ing | She *has been writing* for hours. |
| Simple Past | V₂ | They *wrote* yesterday. |
| Past Continuous | was/were + V-ing | He *was writing* when I called. |
| Past Perfect | had + V₃ | She *had written* before he arrived. |
| Past Perfect Continuous | had been + V-ing | I *had been writing* for an hour before they came. |
| Simple Future | will/shall + V₁ | I *will write* tomorrow. |
| Future Continuous | will be + V-ing | He *will be writing* at 9 a.m. |
| Future Perfect | will have + V₃ | She *will have written* by noon. |
| Future Perfect Continuous | will have been + V-ing | They *will have been writing* for two hours by then. |

**Key Signal Words**:
- Simple past: yesterday, last week, ago, in 2020
- Present perfect: just, already, yet, ever, never, since, for
- Past perfect: before, after, by the time, when (past-before-past)

**JAMB Favourite**: "By the time he arrived, she _____ (leave)."
→ Answer: **had left** (Past Perfect – action completed before another past action)

### Concept 3: Concord (Subject–Verb Agreement) – Most Tested Grammar Topic

**The Core Rule**: A singular subject takes a singular verb; a plural subject takes a plural verb.

**10 Concord Rules Tested in WAEC/JAMB**:

**Rule 1 – Collective Nouns**: Usually singular
- "The committee *has* decided." (not have)
- EXCEPTION: When members act individually → "The jury *were* divided."

**Rule 2 – Compound Subject with "and"**: Plural verb
- "Emeka and Ngozi *are* here."

**Rule 3 – Compound Subject with "or/nor/either…or/neither…nor"**: Verb agrees with nearer subject
- "Either the boys or their teacher *is* coming."
- "Neither the teacher nor the boys *are* coming."

**Rule 4 – Indefinite Pronouns**: Singular verbs
- "Everyone, someone, anyone, no one, each, every" → singular
- "Everyone *is* invited."

**Rule 5 – Uncountable Nouns**: Singular
- "The news *is* good." / "Mathematics *is* difficult."

**Rule 6 – Title, Distance, Amount as Unit**: Singular
- "Five kilometres *is* a long walk."
- "Three hundred naira *was* the price."

**Rule 7 – Relative Clause Agreement**: Verb agrees with antecedent
- "She is one of those students who *work* hard." (who = students)
- "She is the only one of those students who *works* hard." (who = she)

**Rule 8 – There is/There are**: Verb agrees with the noun that follows
- "There *is* a book on the table."
- "There *are* books on the table."

**Rule 9 – Phrases Between Subject and Verb**: Ignore the phrase
- "The box of chocolates *was* left behind." (subject = box, not chocolates)

**Rule 10 – Both, Many, Several, Few, All**: Plural
- "Both of them *are* guilty."

### Concept 4: Common Error Spotting (JAMB/WAEC Sentence Correction)

**Common Grammar Errors Tested**:

| Error Type | Incorrect | Correct |
|-----------|-----------|---------|
| Wrong pronoun case | *Him* and I are friends | *He* and I are friends |
| Wrong tense | Before she came, I *finish* my work | Before she came, I *had finished* my work |
| Double negative | I didn't see *nobody* | I didn't see *anybody* |
| Wrong concord | The students, along with their teacher, *are* happy | The students, along with their teacher, *are* happy ✓ (students = plural) |
| Dangling modifier | *Walking down the road*, a car hit me | *Walking down the road*, I was hit by a car |
| Wrong relative pronoun | The man *which* came | The man *who* came |

## 2. SAMPLE PROBLEMS (5 Increasing Difficulty)

### Problem 1 (EASY – Parts of Speech)
**Q**: Identify the part of speech of the underlined word: "She ran *quickly* to the market."

**Options**:
A) Adjective
B) Verb
C) Adverb ← **CORRECT**
D) Preposition

**Explanation**:
- "Quickly" modifies the verb "ran" → it is an adverb
- Adverbs answer HOW, WHEN, WHERE, WHY about a verb
- Adjectives modify nouns, not verbs

**Key Concept**: Adverb modifies a verb, adjective, or another adverb

---

### Problem 2 (EASY – Tense)
**Q**: Choose the correct tense: "She _____ in Lagos since 2010." (live)

**Options**:
A) lived
B) has lived ← **CORRECT**
C) is living
D) was living

**Explanation**:
- "Since 2010" signals Present Perfect (started in the past, continues now)
- Present Perfect formula: has/have + past participle
- "She has lived" = she started living in 2010 and still lives there now

**Key Concept**: Present Perfect + "since/for" = action started in the past, still ongoing

---

### Problem 3 (MEDIUM – Concord)
**Q**: Choose the correct verb: "Each of the boys _____ a new uniform."

**Options**:
A) have
B) has ← **CORRECT**
C) had had
D) are having

**Explanation**:
- "Each" is an indefinite pronoun → always singular
- Subject = "each" (not boys) → singular verb
- "Each of the boys" → emphasis on individuals, one by one

**Key Concept**: "Each, every, either, neither, anyone, someone" → singular verb

---

### Problem 4 (MEDIUM – Tense Sequence)
**Q**: "By the time Chidi arrived, the teacher _____ the lesson." (finish)

**Options**:
A) finished
B) has finished
C) had finished ← **CORRECT**
D) was finishing

**Explanation**:
- Two past actions: "arrived" (simple past) + finishing was BEFORE "arrived"
- Action completed BEFORE another past action → Past Perfect
- Past Perfect = had + past participle: "had finished"

**Key Concept**: Past-before-past = Past Perfect (had + V₃)

---

### Problem 5 (HARD – Error Spotting)
**Q**: Identify the error: "Either the manager or the staff members is responsible for the missing funds."

**Options**:
A) "Either the manager" should be "Neither the manager"
B) "is responsible" should be "are responsible" ← **CORRECT**
C) "or the staff members" should be "nor the staff members"
D) No error

**Explanation**:
- Rule: Either…or → verb agrees with the NEARER subject
- Nearer subject = "staff members" (plural)
- Plural subject → plural verb: "are responsible"
- "is" is wrong because the nearer noun is plural

**Key Concept**: Either/or, neither/nor → verb agrees with the subject closer to the verb

## 3. COMMON GRAMMAR MISTAKES

| Mistake | Why Wrong | Correct Understanding |
|---------|-----------|------------------------|
| Using "is" after collective nouns acting individually | Collective nouns can be singular or plural | Check context: unified=singular, divided=plural |
| Using present perfect with "ago/last/yesterday" | Those words signal simple past | "I saw him yesterday" not "I have seen him yesterday" |
| Ignoring intervening phrases for concord | "The box of chocolates are" | Subject = "box" (singular); "of chocolates" is just a modifier |
| "Everyone… they" | Everyone = singular | "Everyone should do *his/her* work" |
| Confusing "who" and "which" | "The dog which came" | Use "who" for people; "which/that" for things |

## 5. EXAM QUICK TIPS

**Tip 1**: **"Since/for" = Present Perfect; "ago/last/yesterday" = Simple Past**
- Never use Present Perfect with a specific finished time

**Tip 2**: **Find the TRUE subject, ignore phrases between subject and verb**
- "The box of chocolates *was*…" → subject = box

**Tip 3**: **Either/or, Neither/nor → verb agrees with the CLOSER subject**
- "Either Sola or the boys *are*…" (boys is closer)

**Tip 4**: **Each, every, everyone, someone, no one → always singular verb**
- "Everyone *is*…" never "Everyone *are*…"

**Tip 5**: **Past Perfect = had + V₃ → use when one past action comes before another**
- Trigger words: "before", "after", "by the time", "already"
