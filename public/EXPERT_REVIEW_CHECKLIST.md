# Expert Content Review Checklist

## Purpose
This checklist ensures all generated content meets Akudemy quality standards before deployment to students.

---

## 📋 CONTENT QUALITY REVIEW CHECKLIST

### Section A: Accuracy & Correctness
**Critical - Content must be 100% accurate**

- [ ] **A1: Factual Accuracy**
  - Are all facts, definitions, and explanations correct?
  - Have you verified against official curriculum (WAEC, NECO, JAMB)?
  - Are there any outdated information or misconceptions?
  - **Evidence**: Cite sources or official documents

- [ ] **A2: Formula & Equation Correctness**
  - All mathematical formulas are correct
  - Chemical equations are balanced
  - Biological processes follow proper nomenclature
  - **Example**: CH₄ + 2O₂ → CO₂ + 2H₂O (balanced ✓)

- [ ] **A3: Answer Correctness**
  - Correct answer is actually correct
  - Other options are clearly wrong or good distractors
  - No trick questions without clear justification
  - **Test**: Work through every question yourself

- [ ] **A4: Spelling & Grammar**
  - No spelling errors in questions or answers
  - Grammar is standard Nigerian English or international English (consistent)
  - Subject-verb agreement correct
  - **Pass Rate**: 0 errors in sample of 10 questions

---

### Section B: Clarity & Comprehension
**Important - Students must understand easily**

- [ ] **B1: Question Clarity**
  - Question is unambiguous (only one interpretation)
  - All necessary information is provided
  - No missing context or referents
  - **Test**: Can a student understand without external help?

- [ ] **B2: Explanation Clarity**
  - Explanation answers "Why?" not just "What?"
  - Written at appropriate level (not too simple, not too complex)
  - Uses examples where helpful
  - **Length**: 100-250 words per explanation

- [ ] **B3: Key Concept Identification**
  - Key learning points are clearly highlighted
  - Common mistakes are identified
  - Memory aids (mnemonics) provided where appropriate
  - **Format**: Bold or highlight key terms

- [ ] **B4: Jargon & Terminology**
  - Technical terms are defined or explained
  - Terminology is consistent throughout
  - No unexplained acronyms
  - **Example**: "Photosynthesis (PS)" ✗ | "Photosynthesis is the process..." ✓

---

### Section C: Curriculum Alignment
**Important - Content must be exam-relevant**

- [ ] **C1: JAMB/WAEC/NECO Alignment**
  - Topic is in official exam syllabus
  - Content matches syllabus objectives
  - Emphasis on exam-tested concepts
  - **Reference**: Official syllabus documents

- [ ] **C2: Difficulty Level Appropriateness**
  - Easy questions: Straightforward recall/application
  - Medium questions: Require understanding & analysis
  - Hard questions: Require synthesis & inference
  - **Distribution**: 30% easy, 50% medium, 20% hard (approximately)

- [ ] **C3: Question Type Variety**
  - Includes factual questions (direct from passage/concept)
  - Includes inferential questions (requires reasoning)
  - Includes application questions (real-world use)
  - **JAMB Format**: All multiple-choice with 4 options

- [ ] **C4: Past Paper Consistency**
  - Question style matches real past JAMB papers
  - Difficulty level comparable to official exams
  - Answer options follow JAMB distractors pattern
  - **Validation**: Compare with 2-3 real past papers

---

### Section D: Pedagogical Quality
**Important - Content helps students learn**

- [ ] **D1: Learning Objectives**
  - Study guide clearly states what students will learn
  - Questions test those learning objectives
  - Different difficulty levels target different Bloom's levels
  - **Example**: "Master" vs "Understand" vs "Apply"

- [ ] **D2: Scaffolding & Progression**
  - Easy concepts explained before hard concepts
  - Prerequisites are clear
  - Questions progress from simple to complex
  - **Flow**: Can a student without background understand?

- [ ] **D3: Real-World Relevance**
  - Nigerian context integrated where appropriate
  - Local examples used
  - Cultural sensitivity maintained
  - **Example**: Use Nigerian currency (₦), locations, names

- [ ] **D4: Engagement & Motivation**
  - Content is interesting to secondary students
  - Visual descriptions help imagination
  - Relevance to exam success is clear
  - **Tone**: Professional yet encouraging

---

### Section E: Format & Presentation
**Nice-to-Have - Improves usability**

- [ ] **E1: Formatting Consistency**
  - Headings are consistently formatted
  - Bullet points vs numbered lists used appropriately
  - Bold/italic used for emphasis (not overused)
  - **Standard**: Title, Subtitle, Body, Emphasis markers

- [ ] **E2: Visual Aids**
  - Diagrams/images are relevant and helpful
  - Images are clear and labeled
  - 3D models mentioned for applicable topics
  - **Format**: Links to supporting visuals provided

- [ ] **E3: References & Citations**
  - Sources cited for factual claims
  - Official textbooks referenced
  - Reliable educational sources only
  - **Format**: "According to WAEC syllabus..." or "Source: [name]"

- [ ] **E4: Length & Brevity**
  - Study guide: 2,000-3,000 words
  - Explanation per question: 100-250 words
  - No unnecessary verbosity
  - **Guideline**: "If it can be said in 10 words, don't use 20"

---

### Section F: Inclusivity & Accessibility
**Important - Content works for all students**

- [ ] **F1: Language Accessibility**
  - No unnecessarily complex vocabulary
  - Definitions provided for technical terms
  - Sentence structure is clear
  - **Readability**: Flesch-Kincaid Grade 10-11 level

- [ ] **F2: Cultural Sensitivity**
  - No stereotypes or biases
  - Examples respectful of different backgrounds
  - Gender-neutral language used
  - **Check**: Would all Nigerian students feel included?

- [ ] **F3: Diverse Learning Styles**
  - Visual learners: Diagrams/descriptions
  - Auditory learners: Mention of videos
  - Kinesthetic learners: Hands-on examples
  - **Coverage**: At least 2 learning modalities represented

---

### Section G: Question Quality Checklist
**Critical - Apply to every question**

For each question, verify:

- [ ] **Q1: One Best Answer**
  - Only ONE option is clearly best
  - No debate between two answers
  - Correct answer is defensible

- [ ] **Q2: Plausible Distractors**
  - Wrong options are plausible (common mistakes)
  - Not obviously wrong
  - Test common misconceptions
  - **Example**: For algebraic questions, include sign errors as distractors

- [ ] **Q3: No Trick Questions**
  - Unless explicitly about careful reading
  - Tricks are fair and educational
  - "Gotcha" questions avoided
  - **Principle**: Test knowledge, not test-taking tricks

- [ ] **Q4: Independent Questions**
  - Question doesn't depend on previous question answers
  - Each question stands alone
  - No repeated information across questions
  - **Test**: Can questions be asked in any order?

- [ ] **Q5: Appropriate Difficulty**
  - Label matches actual difficulty
  - Difficulty increases across set appropriately
  - **Check**: Compare to past papers of same difficulty

## 📊 SCORING SYSTEM

### Quality Scores (Per Item)

**Accuracy (A)** - Critically Important
- All sub-items must be checked: 5 points
- 3-4 sub-items checked: 3 points
- <3 sub-items checked: 0 points (REJECT)

**Clarity (B)** - Very Important
- All sub-items checked: 5 points
- 3 sub-items checked: 3 points
- <3 sub-items checked: 0 points (REJECT)

**Alignment (C)** - Very Important
- All sub-items checked: 5 points
- 3 sub-items checked: 3 points
- <3 sub-items checked: 0 points (REJECT)

**Pedagogy (D)** - Important
- All sub-items checked: 5 points
- 2-3 sub-items checked: 3 points
- <2 sub-items checked: 1 point

**Format (E)** - Nice-to-Have
- All sub-items checked: 3 points
- 2 sub-items checked: 2 points
- 1 sub-item checked: 1 point

**Inclusivity (F)** - Important
- All sub-items checked: 3 points
- 2 sub-items checked: 2 points
- <2 sub-items checked: 1 point

**Questions (G)** - Critical
- All sub-items per question: 2 points per Q
- 4 sub-items per question: 1.5 points per Q
- <4 sub-items per question: 0 points (REJECT)

---

### Overall Grade

**PASS (Ready to Deploy)**
- Score ≥ 28/35 (80%+)
- All Critical sections (A, B, C, G) have maximum points
- No REJECT verdicts

**REVISE (Fix & Resubmit)**
- Score 20-27/35 (57-77%)
- Has some issues but fixable
- May have 1-2 sub-items failed in important sections

**REJECT (Do Not Deploy)**
- Score <20/35 (<57%)
- Critical accuracy issues
- Cannot be fixed without major rewrite
- Multiple sections have failed sub-items

## 🎯 QUALITY ASSURANCE PROCESS

1. **First Review** (Content Creator)
   - Creator self-reviews using this checklist
   - Catches obvious errors before submission

2. **Second Review** (Subject Matter Expert)
   - Expert reviews accuracy (Section A-C)
   - Provides detailed feedback
   - Approves or requests revisions

3. **Third Review** (Pedagogy Lead)
   - Reviews teaching quality (Section D-F)
   - Ensures student understanding
   - Approves or suggests improvements

4. **Final QA** (Quality Assurance Team)
   - Final check before deployment
   - Spot-check random samples
   - Ensure all checkboxes ticked

5. **Deployment**
   - Content goes live to students
   - Monitor engagement & feedback
   - Log any issues for future versions

---

## 📞 ESCALATION PATH

**If question arises about:**
- **Accuracy**: Escalate to Subject Matter Expert
- **Teaching method**: Escalate to Pedagogy Lead
- **Format/Presentation**: Escalate to Content Team Lead
- **Major disputes**: Escalate to Content Manager
