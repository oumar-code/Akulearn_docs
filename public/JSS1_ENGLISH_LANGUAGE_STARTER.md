## 1. KEY CONCEPTS (Master These)

### Concept 1: Parts of Speech (The 8 Parts)

| Part of Speech | Definition | Examples |
|----------------|-----------|---------|
| **Noun** | Name of a person, place, thing or idea | Amina, Lagos, book, freedom |
| **Pronoun** | Replaces a noun | he, she, it, they, we, I |
| **Verb** | Action or state of being | run, is, write, have |
| **Adjective** | Describes a noun | tall, red, many, beautiful |
| **Adverb** | Describes a verb, adjective or another adverb | quickly, very, always, here |
| **Preposition** | Shows relationship between nouns | in, on, at, between, under |
| **Conjunction** | Joins words or clauses | and, but, or, because, although |
| **Interjection** | Expresses emotion | Oh!, Wow!, Alas! |

**Memory trick** — NAVAPCI: Noun, Adverb, Verb, Adjective, Pronoun, Conjunction, Interjection

**Nouns — types**:
- **Common**: boy, city, dog
- **Proper** (capital letter): Abuja, Emeka, Monday
- **Abstract**: love, courage, wisdom
- **Collective**: a flock of birds, a class of students, a pack of wolves

### Concept 2: Tenses — Present, Past and Future

**Simple Tenses**:
| Tense | Form | Example |
|-------|------|---------|
| Simple Present | base verb / base + s/es | She **reads** every day. |
| Simple Past | verb + -ed (regular) OR irregular | He **went** home. |
| Simple Future | will + base verb | They **will study** tomorrow. |

**Common Irregular Verbs** (must memorise):
| Base | Past Simple | Past Participle |
|------|------------|----------------|
| go | went | gone |
| come | came | come |
| write | wrote | written |
| see | saw | seen |
| take | took | taken |
| bring | brought | brought |
| eat | ate | eaten |
| give | gave | given |

**Subject–Verb Agreement**:
- Singular subject → verb + s/es: *The boy **runs**.*
- Plural subject → base verb: *The boys **run**.*
- "The news **is** good" (news is singular)

### Concept 3: Reading Comprehension — Strategies

**Before reading**:
1. Read the questions first — know what to look for
2. Skim the passage for the main idea (title, first and last sentences)

**During reading**:
1. Underline key information
2. Note the tone (happy, sad, angry, persuasive)
3. Identify the main idea vs supporting details

**Answering questions**:
- **Literal** questions → find the answer directly in the text
- **Inferential** questions → use clues from the text to reason the answer
- **Vocabulary** questions → use context clues around the unfamiliar word

**Types of passages**:
- Narrative (tells a story)
- Expository (gives information)
- Argumentative / Persuasive (makes a case for something)

### Concept 4: Letter Writing

**Formal Letter Format**:
```
Your address
         (leave a line)
The Recipient's address
         (leave a line)
Date
         (leave a line)
Salutation: Dear Sir/Madam, or Dear Mr/Mrs [Name],
         (leave a line)
Subject heading (underlined): APPLICATION FOR ADMISSION
         (leave a line)
Body: paragraphs (introduction, main content, conclusion)
         (leave a line)
Complimentary close: Yours faithfully, (when you wrote "Dear Sir/Madam")
                     Yours sincerely, (when you used the person's name)
         (leave a line)
Your signature
Your name (printed)
```

**Informal Letter** (to a friend or relative):
- Your address and date (top right)
- Salutation: Dear [First name],
- Friendly, conversational tone
- Close: Yours affectionately, / Your friend,

**Common mistakes**:
- Using "Yours faithfully" when you addressed by name (should be "Yours sincerely")
- No subject heading in a formal letter
- Mixing formal and informal tone

### Concept 5: Punctuation and Spelling Essentials

**Must-know punctuation**:
| Mark | Use | Example |
|------|-----|---------|
| Full stop (.) | End of statement | She left. |
| Comma (,) | Pause, list, clause | I bought bread, butter and eggs. |
| Question mark (?) | End of question | Where are you? |
| Exclamation mark (!) | Exclamation | What a day! |
| Apostrophe (') | Possession / contraction | John's book / it's = it is |
| Colon (:) | Introduce a list | I need three things: sugar, milk, eggs. |
| Semicolon (;) | Join related clauses | It is hot; wear light clothes. |

**Commonly confused words**:
| Pair | Difference |
|------|-----------|
| there / their / they're | place / belonging / they are |
| its / it's | belonging / it is |
| affect / effect | verb (to influence) / noun (result) |
| lose / loose | not win or misplace / not tight |
| your / you're | belonging / you are |

## 2. SAMPLE PROBLEMS (5 Increasing Difficulty)

### Problem 1 (EASY — Parts of Speech)
**Q**: Identify the part of speech of the underlined word: "She **runs** very fast."

**Answer**: **Verb** (shows action)

**Explanation**: "runs" tells what the subject (she) does.

---

### Problem 2 (EASY — Tenses)
**Q**: Change to past tense: "He **writes** a letter."

**Answer**: "He **wrote** a letter."

**Explanation**: "write" is an irregular verb → past tense is "wrote" (not "writed").

---

### Problem 3 (MEDIUM — Comprehension)
**Q** (based on a sample passage): "The fire spread rapidly because the walls were made of dry wood."

"What caused the fire to spread?"

**Answer**: The walls were made of dry wood.

**Explanation**: Literal question — answer is stated directly in the passage.

---

### Problem 4 (MEDIUM — Letter Writing)
**Q**: You are writing a formal letter to your school principal requesting permission to organise a debate. What complimentary close should you use?

**Options**: A) Yours affectionately   B) Yours sincerely   C) Yours faithfully   D) Love

**Answer**: **C — Yours faithfully** (because you addressed "Dear Principal" without using a specific name, i.e., "Dear Sir/Madam" style)

---

### Problem 5 (HARD — Error Correction)
**Q**: Identify and correct the error: "The students has submitted their assignments."

**Answer**: "The students **have** submitted their assignments."

**Explanation**: "students" is plural, so the verb must be "have" (not "has").

## 3. COMMON MISTAKES IN JSS1 ENGLISH

| Mistake | Correction |
|---------|-----------|
| Using "Yours faithfully" when name is given | Use "Yours sincerely" when name is used in salutation |
| "He don't go to school" | "He **doesn't** go to school" |
| "I buyed a book" | "I **bought** a book" (irregular verb) |
| Confusing "their/there/they're" | their = belonging; there = place; they're = they are |
| Omitting the subject heading in formal letters | Always include and underline the heading |

## 5. EXAM QUICK TIPS

**Tip 1**: **Read comprehension questions BEFORE the passage** — saves time, focuses attention

**Tip 2**: **For tense questions** — look at time words (yesterday/now/tomorrow) as context clues

**Tip 3**: **For subject–verb agreement** — ignore words between subject and verb; find the core subject

**Tip 4**: **Letter writing** — use the correct close: *faithfully* (unknown recipient) / *sincerely* (named recipient)

**Tip 5**: **For vocabulary in context** — try substituting each option into the sentence; pick the one that makes sense
