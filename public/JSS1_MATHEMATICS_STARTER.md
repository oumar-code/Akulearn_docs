## 1. KEY CONCEPTS (Master These)

### Concept 1: Whole Numbers and Place Value

**Place Value Table**:
```
Millions | Hundred-Thousands | Ten-Thousands | Thousands | Hundreds | Tens | Units
   1,000,000 |      100,000     |     10,000    |   1,000   |   100   |  10  |   1
```

**Example**: Write 3,047,205 in words
→ Three million, forty-seven thousand, two hundred and five

**Rounding Numbers**:
- Round 4,673 to the nearest hundred → 4,700
- Round 4,673 to the nearest thousand → 5,000
- Rule: if the next digit is 5 or above → round up; below 5 → round down

**Why Important**: Place value underlies every arithmetic operation — wrong place value = wrong answer

---

### Concept 2: Basic Operations — BODMAS Rule

**BODMAS** (order of operations):
```
B — Brackets
O — Of (powers / roots)
D — Division
M — Multiplication
A — Addition
S — Subtraction
```

**Example**: Calculate 3 + 4 × 2
- Multiplication first: 4 × 2 = 8
- Then addition: 3 + 8 = **11** (NOT 14)

**With brackets**: (3 + 4) × 2 = 7 × 2 = **14**

**Long Multiplication**:
```
  346
×  24
------
 1384   (346 × 4)
+6920   (346 × 20)
------
 8304
```

**Long Division**:
```
672 ÷ 14:
  14 × 4 = 56  → 67 − 56 = 11 → bring down 2 → 112 ÷ 14 = 8
  Answer: 48
```

### Concept 3: Fractions, Decimals and Percentages

**Types of Fractions**:
| Type | Definition | Example |
|------|-----------|---------|
| Proper fraction | Numerator < Denominator | 3/5 |
| Improper fraction | Numerator > Denominator | 7/4 |
| Mixed number | Whole number + proper fraction | 1¾ |

**Converting**:
- 7/4 as mixed number: 7 ÷ 4 = 1 remainder 3 → **1¾**
- 1¾ as improper: (1 × 4) + 3 = 7 → **7/4**

**Adding Fractions** (find LCM of denominators):
```
1/3 + 1/4 = 4/12 + 3/12 = 7/12
```

**Percentages**:
- 25% of 80 = (25/100) × 80 = **20**
- Express 15 as % of 60 = (15/60) × 100 = **25%**

**Fraction → Decimal → Percentage**:
```
1/4 = 0.25 = 25%
1/2 = 0.5  = 50%
3/4 = 0.75 = 75%
```

### Concept 4: Introduction to Basic Algebra

**A variable** is a letter that stands for an unknown number.

**Simplifying expressions**:
- Collect like terms: 3x + 5x = 8x
- 4a + 2b − a + 3b = 3a + 5b

**Solving simple equations**:
```
x + 7 = 12
x = 12 − 7
x = 5       (check: 5 + 7 = 12 ✓)

3y = 18
y = 18 ÷ 3
y = 6
```

**Word problems → algebra**:
- "I think of a number, add 9, and get 15. Find the number."
- Let the number = n: n + 9 = 15 → n = 6

---

### Concept 5: Factors, Multiples, HCF and LCM

**Factors**: all numbers that divide exactly into a given number
- Factors of 12: 1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 12

**Multiples**: the times-table values of a number
- Multiples of 4: 4, 8, 12, 16, 20 …

**HCF** (Highest Common Factor) — find the largest factor shared:
- HCF of 12 and 18 = 6

**LCM** (Lowest Common Multiple) — find the smallest multiple shared:
- LCM of 4 and 6 = 12

**Prime Factorisation** (factor tree):
```
  36
 /  \
4    9
/ \ / \
2 2 3  3
36 = 2² × 3²
```

## 2. SAMPLE PROBLEMS (5 Increasing Difficulty)

### Problem 1 (EASY — BODMAS)
**Q**: Evaluate 12 ÷ 4 + 3 × 2

**Options**: A) 9   B) 9   C) 3   D) 9

**Solution**:
```
Division and Multiplication first (left to right):
12 ÷ 4 = 3
3 × 2  = 6
Then addition: 3 + 6 = 9
```
**Answer**: **9**

**Key Concept**: Division and multiplication before addition and subtraction

---

### Problem 2 (EASY — Fractions)
**Q**: What is ¾ + ½?

**Options**: A) 4/6   B) 5/4   C) 1¼   D) 2/7

**Solution**:
```
LCM of 4 and 2 is 4
¾ + 2/4 = 5/4 = 1¼
```
**Answer**: **1¼**

---

### Problem 3 (MEDIUM — Percentages)
**Q**: A bag costs ₦2,400. If the price is increased by 15%, what is the new price?

**Solution**:
```
15% of 2,400 = (15/100) × 2,400 = 360
New price = 2,400 + 360 = ₦2,760
```
**Answer**: **₦2,760**

---

### Problem 4 (MEDIUM — Simple Equations)
**Q**: Solve for x: 2x + 5 = 17

**Solution**:
```
2x = 17 − 5
2x = 12
x  = 6
Check: 2(6) + 5 = 17 ✓
```
**Answer**: **x = 6**

---

### Problem 5 (HARD — HCF/LCM Word Problem)
**Q**: Three buses leave a station every 12, 15 and 20 minutes respectively. If they all leave together at 7:00 am, when will they next all leave together?

**Solution**:
```
LCM of 12, 15, 20:
12 = 2² × 3
15 = 3 × 5
20 = 2² × 5
LCM = 2² × 3 × 5 = 60 minutes
They next leave together 60 minutes later → 8:00 am
```
**Answer**: **8:00 am**

## 3. COMMON MISTAKES IN JSS1 MATHEMATICS

| Mistake | Why Wrong | Correct Approach |
|---------|-----------|-----------------|
| Adding before multiplying | Ignoring BODMAS | Do M/D before A/S |
| Adding fractions straight across (1/3 + 1/4 = 2/7) | Denominators must match | Find LCM first |
| Confusing HCF and LCM | Wrong operation chosen | HCF = divide shared; LCM = smallest common multiple |
| Forgetting to check equation solution | Arithmetic slip goes undetected | Always substitute back |
| Mixing unlike terms (3x + 2 = 5x) | Variables and numbers aren't like terms | Only collect identical variable terms |

## 5. EXAM QUICK TIPS

**Tip 1**: **Write out BODMAS before every multi-step calculation**

**Tip 2**: **Always find LCM before adding/subtracting fractions**

**Tip 3**: **Check equation solutions by substituting back**

**Tip 4**: **In percentage problems, identify the "whole" — it is always the denominator**

**Tip 5**: **For word problems, write a let statement (let x = …) before forming the equation**
