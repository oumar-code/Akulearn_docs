    Network Gateway Pilot Deployment Plan¶

    Executive Summary¶

    Phased rollout plan for eSIM and physical SIM network gateway services, progressing from limited pilot (100 users) to general availability. Includes rollout schedule, success metrics, monitoring setup, and rollback procedures.

    Pilot Start Date: February 10, 2026
    General Availability Target: April 1, 2026
    Total Duration: 7 weeks (pilot) + 2 weeks (ramp-up)

    Rollout Phases¶

    Phase A: Internal Alpha (Week 1)¶

    Duration: February 10-16, 2026 (1 week)
    Participants: 20 Akulearn employees and contractors

    Objectives: - Validate end-to-end activation workflows - Stress-test support runbook with real users - Identify critical bugs before external pilot

    Device Distribution: - 12 eSIM-capable smartphones (internal staff) - 8 physical SIMs for feature phones (field agents)

    Success Criteria: - ✅ Activation success rate: ≥90% - ✅ Zero P1 incidents (critical outages) - ✅ Support team resolves 80% of issues on first contact - ✅ No billing discrepancies

    Go/No-Go Decision: February 16, 2026 (end of week) - Go: Proceed to Pilot Beta if all success criteria met - No-Go: Extend alpha by 1 week; fix critical issues

    Phase B: Pilot Beta (Week 2-5)¶

    Duration: February 17 - March 16, 2026 (4 weeks)
    Participants: 100 external users across 3 regions

    Region Distribution: | Region | Coverage Type | Users | Device Mix | |--------|---------------|-------|------------| | Lagos (A) | Aku Edge Full | 40 | 25 eSIM, 15 pSIM | | Ibadan (B) | Partial Aku Edge | 40 | 20 eSIM, 20 pSIM | | Osogbo (C) | MNO Fallback Only | 20 | 10 eSIM, 10 pSIM |

    Objectives: - Validate network gateway performance in real-world conditions - Measure user satisfaction and identify UX friction - Tune policy enforcement and QoS thresholds - Validate billing accuracy over 4-week period

    User Selection Criteria: - Active Akulearn learners (≥5 lessons completed) - Diverse age range (16-45 years) - Mix of device types (smartphones and feature phones) - Geographic diversity (urban, semi-urban, rural) - Willingness to provide feedback via weekly surveys

    Pilot Onboarding: 1. Week 1 (Feb 17-23): Onboard 50 users (Lagos + Ibadan) 2. Week 2 (Feb 24-Mar 2): Onboard remaining 50 users (Ibadan + Osogbo) 3. Week 3-4 (Mar 3-16): Monitor usage, collect feedback, resolve issues 4. Week 5 (Mar 17-23): Final surveys and data analysis

    Success Criteria: - ✅ Activation success rate: ≥95% - ✅ User satisfaction score: ≥4.0/5.0 - ✅ Daily active usage: ≥70% of pilot users - ✅ Support ticket volume: <5 per 100 users per week - ✅ Network handover success (Aku ↔ MNO): ≥90% - ✅ Policy enforcement accuracy: 100% (no billing errors) - ✅ Zero P1 incidents, <3 P2 incidents

    Weekly Milestones: - Week 2 (Feb 24): 25 users activated, no critical issues - Week 3 (Mar 3): 50 users activated, satisfaction score ≥3.5 - Week 4 (Mar 10): 75 users activated, support ticket trend declining - Week 5 (Mar 17): 100 users activated, all success criteria met

    Go/No-Go Decision: March 23, 2026 - Go: Proceed to Limited GA if all success criteria met - No-Go: Extend pilot by 2 weeks; address deficiencies

    Phase C: Limited General Availability (Week 6-7)¶

    Duration: March 24 - April 6, 2026 (2 weeks)
    Participants: Gradual ramp-up to 1,000 users

    Ramp-Up Schedule: - Week 6 (Mar 24-30): 500 new activations (target: 100/day) - Week 7 (Mar 31-Apr 6): 500 new activations (target: 100/day) - Total: 1,000 active users by end of week 7

    Objectives: - Validate system scalability under increased load - Monitor backend performance (API latency, HLR/HSS capacity) - Ensure support team can handle increased ticket volume - Prepare infrastructure for full GA launch

    Success Criteria: - ✅ Activation success rate: ≥97% - ✅ API P95 latency: <2 seconds - ✅ HLR/HSS CPU utilization: <70% - ✅ Support first-contact resolution rate: ≥80% - ✅ Zero P1 incidents, <2 P2 incidents per week

    Go/No-Go Decision: April 6, 2026 - Go: Proceed to Full GA on April 7 - No-Go: Pause new activations; scale infrastructure; resolve issues

    Phase D: Full General Availability (Week 8+)¶

    Launch Date: April 7, 2026
    Participants: All Akulearn users (unrestricted)

    Ramp-Up Strategy: - Week 8 (Apr 7-13): 2,000 new activations (target: 300/day) - Week 9 (Apr 14-20): 3,000 new activations (target: 400/day) - Week 10+ (Apr 21+): Uncapped growth (monitor capacity thresholds)

    Capacity Thresholds: | Metric | Threshold | Action if Exceeded | |--------|-----------|-------------------| | API requests/min | 800 | Scale API servers horizontally | | HLR/HSS CPU | 80% | Add HLR instance or upgrade | | PGW sessions | 5,000 concurrent | Add PGW capacity | | Support ticket queue | >50 open | Add support staff or deflect to FAQ | | Activation failure rate | >5% | Pause new activations; investigate root cause |

    Success Metrics (Ongoing): - Activation success rate: ≥98% - User retention: ≥85% active after 30 days - Average data usage: 300-500 MB/user/month - Support ticket volume: <2 per 100 users per week - Billing accuracy: 100% (zero false charges)

    Success Metrics & KPIs¶

    1. Activation Metrics¶

    Primary KPIs: - Activation Success Rate: (Successful activations / Total attempts) × 100 - Target: ≥95% (pilot), ≥98% (GA) - Measurement: Real-time via Grafana dashboard - Alert: <95% triggers Slack notification

    • Time to Activate: P95 latency from registration to network attach
    • Target: <90 seconds (eSIM), <120 seconds (pSIM)
    • Measurement: Prometheus histogram metrics

    • Alert: P95 >180 seconds triggers investigation

    • Activation Abandonment Rate: Users who start but don't complete activation

    • Target: <10%
    • Measurement: Funnel analysis (registration → QR scan → profile download → network attach)
    • Alert: >15% indicates UX friction

    Secondary KPIs: - QR code scan success rate: ≥90% - Manual entry usage rate: <15% (indicates QR scan issues) - Profile download completion: ≥95%

    2. Network Performance Metrics¶

    Primary KPIs: - Network Attach Success Rate: Devices successfully registering to network - Target: ≥95% - Measurement: attach.success events / (attach.success + attach.failed) × 100 - Alert: <90% triggers network ops escalation

    • Aku Edge Selection Rate: % of time users on Aku network (when in coverage)
    • Target: ≥70% in Region A, ≥50% in Region B
    • Measurement: Network status queries aggregated daily

    • Alert: <60% in Region A suggests config issue

    • Handover Success Rate: Seamless transition between Aku Edge ↔ MNO

    • Target: ≥90%
    • Measurement: handover.success events vs. handover.failed
    • Alert: <85% triggers network ops review

    Secondary KPIs: - Average attach latency: P95 <90 seconds - Network registration drop rate: <2% (unexpected disconnects) - Roaming policy application latency: P95 <15 seconds

    3. Policy & Billing Metrics¶

    Primary KPIs: - Policy Enforcement Accuracy: Zero-rated content not billed - Target: 100% (no tolerance for billing errors) - Measurement: Audit zero-rated traffic in billing records - Alert: Any false billing triggers immediate investigation

    • Billing Reconciliation Accuracy: Backend usage matches billing system
    • Target: 100% (±1% tolerance for rounding)
    • Measurement: Daily reconciliation job comparing data-usage API vs. billing DB
    • Alert: >1% discrepancy triggers billing team review

    Secondary KPIs: - Policy push latency: P95 <10 seconds - QoS tier compliance: Speed tests within ±10% of tier limits - Voice call quality (MOS score): ≥4.0 for voice tier users

    4. User Experience Metrics¶

    Primary KPIs: - User Satisfaction Score (CSAT): Survey rating after activation - Target: ≥4.0/5.0 - Measurement: In-app survey sent 24 hours post-activation - Alert: <3.5 indicates serious UX issues

    • Daily Active Users (DAU): % of pilot users with network activity
    • Target: ≥70%
    • Measurement: Users with attach or data usage events in last 24 hours

    • Alert: <50% suggests churn or network issues

    • Net Promoter Score (NPS): Likelihood to recommend (0-10 scale)

    • Target: ≥40 (promoters - detractors)
    • Measurement: Survey sent at week 4 of pilot
    • Alert: <20 indicates dissatisfaction

    Secondary KPIs: - Activation completion time (user perspective): Target <5 minutes - Support contact rate: <10% of users contact support in first week - Feature phone user satisfaction: ≥3.8/5.0 (expected slightly lower due to USSD UX)

    5. Operational Metrics¶

    Primary KPIs: - Support First-Contact Resolution Rate: Issues resolved without escalation - Target: ≥80% - Measurement: Support ticket system (resolved vs. escalated) - Alert: <70% indicates runbook gaps or complex issues

    • Support Ticket Volume: Tickets per 100 users per week
    • Target: <5
    • Measurement: Support portal query by pilot cohort

    • Alert: >10 indicates systemic issue (e.g., app bug, network outage)

    • System Uptime: Availability of backend APIs and network services

    • Target: 99.5% (pilot), 99.9% (GA)
    • Measurement: Uptime monitoring (Pingdom, StatusCake)
    • Alert: <99% triggers incident response

    Secondary KPIs: - Mean time to resolution (MTTR): <2 hours for P2 incidents - Field agent productivity: 20 SIM activations per agent per day - Backend API error rate: <0.5%

    Prometheus Alerts¶

    Critical Alerts (PagerDuty):

    - alert: ActivationFailureSpike
  expr: rate(activation_failures_total[5m]) > 0.05
  for: 5m
  labels:
    severity: critical
  annotations:
    summary: "Activation failure rate >5% for 5 minutes"

- alert: HLROutage
  expr: up{job="hlr"} == 0
  for: 1m
  labels:
    severity: critical
  annotations:
    summary: "HLR/HSS unreachable"

- alert: BillingDiscrepancy
  expr: abs(data_usage_backend_mb - data_usage_billing_mb) > 10
  for: 10m
  labels:
    severity: critical
  annotations:
    summary: "Billing discrepancy >10 MB detected"

    High-Priority Alerts (Slack #network-ops):

    - alert: AttachSuccessLow
  expr: rate(attach_success_total[10m]) / rate(attach_attempts_total[10m]) < 0.90
  for: 10m
  labels:
    severity: high
  annotations:
    summary: "Network attach success rate <90%"

- alert: PolicyPushLatencyHigh
  expr: histogram_quantile(0.95, policy_push_duration_seconds) > 15
  for: 5m
  labels:
    severity: high
  annotations:
    summary: "Policy push P95 latency >15 seconds"

    Medium-Priority Alerts (Email):

    - alert: SupportTicketVolumeHigh
  expr: support_tickets_open > 50
  for: 1h
  labels:
    severity: medium
  annotations:
    summary: "Support ticket queue >50 open tickets"

    Event Bus Monitoring¶

    Critical Events (Real-Time): - profile.activation.failed — Trigger alert if >5 failures in 5 minutes - billing.discrepancy.detected — Immediate notification to billing team - hlr.provisioning.timeout — Escalate to network ops

    Audit Events (Daily Review): - swap.completed — Verify IMEI updates propagated correctly - iccid.suspended — Cross-check against lost/stolen reports - policy.pushed — Validate QoS tiers applied

    Rollback Procedures¶

    Rollback Trigger Conditions¶

    Initiate rollback if any of the following occur during pilot: - ❌ Activation success rate drops below 80% for >1 hour - ❌ P1 incident (HLR outage, billing failure) lasting >4 hours - ❌ Policy enforcement failure causing false billing (≥10 users affected) - ❌ User satisfaction score drops below 3.0 - ❌ Security breach or unauthorized access to SIM management APIs

    Rollback Plan A: Pause New Activations¶

    Scenario: Issues affecting new activations but existing users unaffected

    Steps: 1. Disable new SIM registration in Field Agent App (feature flag: allow_new_registrations = false) 2. Post notice on status page: "New SIM activations temporarily unavailable" 3. Allow existing users to continue using service 4. Investigate and fix root cause 5. Re-enable registrations after validation

    Downtime: 0 for existing users; 2-24 hours for new activations

    Rollback Plan B: Deactivate Pilot Cohort¶

    Scenario: Critical network or billing issues affecting all users

    Steps: 1. Mark all pilot ICCIDs as SUSPENDED in backend 2. Devices lose network registration within 10 seconds 3. Send SMS to pilot users: "Service temporarily unavailable for maintenance" 4. Fix critical issue (e.g., restore HLR, fix policy engine) 5. Re-activate pilot cohort: PATCH /psim/iccids/bulk with status: ACTIVE 6. Monitor for 1 hour to ensure stability

    Downtime: 2-8 hours (depending on issue severity)

    Rollback Plan C: Full Rollback to Pre-Pilot State¶

    Scenario: Catastrophic failure requiring complete rollback

    Steps: 1. Deactivate all pilot SIMs (Plan B) 2. Roll back backend services to pre-pilot version (blue-green deployment) 3. Restore database from pre-pilot snapshot (data loss: max 24 hours) 4. Notify pilot users: Service discontinued; refund any charges 5. Conduct post-mortem and rebuild pilot plan

    Downtime: 8-48 hours
    Data Loss: Up to 24 hours of usage data

    Risk Mitigation Strategies¶

    Risk 1: HLR/HSS Overload¶

    Likelihood: Medium
    Impact: High (prevents all activations)

    Mitigation: - Pre-pilot load testing (see validation-test-plan.md, Section 4.2) - HLR horizontal scaling capability (add instance in <30 minutes) - Circuit breaker to prevent cascading failures - Backup HLR endpoint (failover in <5 minutes)

    Contingency: - If HLR overloaded during pilot, immediately reduce activation rate - Temporarily limit new registrations to 50/day until scaling complete

    Risk 2: Policy Enforcement Failure (False Billing)¶

    Likelihood: Low
    Impact: Critical (reputation damage, refunds)

    Mitigation: - Daily billing reconciliation audit - Pre-pilot policy validation tests (100% accuracy required) - Alerts on any zero-rated traffic appearing in billing records - Manual review of first 100 pilot activations

    Contingency: - If false billing detected, immediately suspend policy pushes - Manually review and refund all affected users within 24 hours - Root cause analysis and fix before resuming pilot

    Risk 3: User Churn (Poor Satisfaction)¶

    Likelihood: Medium
    Impact: Medium (pilot success in jeopardy)

    Mitigation: - Weekly user surveys to detect dissatisfaction early - Dedicated support hotline for pilot users (ext 4455) - Proactive outreach to inactive users (DAU <50%) - Incentives for participation (bonus data bundles)

    Contingency: - If CSAT drops below 3.0, pause onboarding new users - Conduct focus groups to identify UX friction points - Implement quick-win improvements (e.g., clearer activation instructions)

    Risk 4: Support Team Overwhelmed¶

    Likelihood: Medium
    Impact: Medium (delays resolution, frustration)

    Mitigation: - Support team training 2 weeks before pilot (field-operations-runbook.md) - On-call engineering escalation path - Self-service FAQ and video tutorials - Support ticket auto-routing by issue type

    Contingency: - If ticket volume exceeds 10/100 users/week, add 2 temporary support staff - Deflect common issues to FAQ (e.g., "How to activate eSIM") - Extend support hours to 24/7 during first 2 weeks

    Communication Plan¶

    Internal Stakeholders¶

    Weekly Pilot Standups: - Attendees: Product manager, engineering lead, QA lead, support manager, network ops - Agenda: KPI review, open issues, upcoming milestones, go/no-go checkpoints - Duration: 30 minutes - Day/Time: Mondays 9 AM

    Monthly Executive Briefs: - Attendees: CEO, CTO, CFO, product VP - Format: Email summary + optional deep-dive session - Content: Pilot progress, financial impact, GA readiness, risks

    External Stakeholders (Pilot Users)¶

    Pre-Pilot Communication: - Email 1 week before activation: "You've been selected for Akulearn SIM pilot!" - Content: What to expect, how to activate, support contact - Include FAQ link and activation video tutorial

    During Pilot: - Weekly SMS: "How's your Akulearn SIM experience? Reply YES if satisfied, NO if issues" - Week 2 survey: "Rate your activation experience (1-5)" - Week 4 survey: "Overall satisfaction and NPS score"

    Post-Pilot: - Thank you email with feedback summary - Offer to continue service at discounted rate (pilot incentive) - Invitation to provide testimonial or case study

    Public Communication¶

    Status Page: https://status.akulearn.com/network-gateway - Green: All systems operational - Yellow: Elevated latency or minor issues - Red: Service disruption or outage

    Blog Post (Launch Announcement): - Published on April 7, 2026 (GA launch) - Title: "Introducing Akulearn Connectivity: Learn Anywhere, Anytime" - Content: Product overview, benefits, how to get started, pricing

    Social Media: - Twitter/X: Daily tips and tricks during pilot - Instagram: User testimonials and activation tutorials - Facebook: Community group for pilot participants
