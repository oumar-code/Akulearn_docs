## Content


### 3.1: First Republic (1960–1966)

*Duration: 22 minutes*

Parliamentary system: President (ceremonial) + Prime Minister (executive). Major parties: NPC (north), NCNC (east), Action Group (west). Issues: census controversies, election disputes, regional tensions, January 1966 coup.


### 3.2: Civil War (1967–1970)

*Duration: 23 minutes*

Causes: ethnic/regional mistrust post-coups, pogroms, failure of Aburi Accord, declaration of Republic of Biafra (May 1967). War phases: initial secession, federal counteroffensive, blockades. Consequences: casualties, displacement, economic disruption; post-war policy of 'No Victor, No Vanquished', reconciliation, 3Rs (Reconstruction, Rehabilitation, Reconciliation).


### 3.3: Military Rule and Transitions

*Duration: 22 minutes*

Key regimes: Gowon (1966–75) post-war reconstruction; Murtala/Obasanjo (1975–79) reforms and 1979 handover; Buhari (1983–85) anti-corruption/War Against Indiscipline; Babangida (1985–93) SAP, annulled June 12 election; Abacha (1993–98) authoritarian rule; Abdulsalami (1998–99) transition.


### 3.4: Return to Democracy and Contemporary Issues

*Duration: 23 minutes*

1999 Fourth Republic: Constitution (presidential system); elected President Obasanjo (1999). Subsequent elections (2003, 2007, 2011, 2015 power alternation, 2019, 2023). Issues: governance, corruption, security (insurgency, banditry), economic diversification, federal character principle, devolution debates.


## Worked Examples


### Example 3.1: Identifying a Cause of the Civil War

**Context**: Failure of the Aburi Accord led to mistrust.

**Problem**: How did this contribute to the conflict?

**Solution**:
```
The breakdown of agreed power-sharing/restructuring increased distrust between federal and eastern leaders, prompting secession declaration and escalation to war.
```

**Skills Tested**: Cause-effect analysis, Conflict reasoning


### Example 3.2: Policy Under Military Rule

**Context**: Babangida introduced Structural Adjustment Programme (SAP).

**Problem**: What was one goal and one criticism of SAP?

**Solution**:
```
Goal: liberalize economy, reduce state spending, encourage private sector. Criticism: led to hardship via devaluation, subsidy removal, social unrest.
```

**Skills Tested**: Policy evaluation, Economic-social impacts


### Example 3.3: Democratic Transition

**Context**: 1999 handover to civilian rule.

**Problem**: Name one factor that made the transition possible.

**Solution**:
```
Abdulsalami Abubakar's short transition program, constitution drafting, elections; domestic/international pressure for civilian rule; fatigue with military governance.
```

**Skills Tested**: Transition analysis, Political change factors


## Practice Problems

**3.1.B**: When did Nigeria gain independence?

> **Answer**: October 1, 1960.

> **Explanation**: Key date recall.


**3.2.B**: Name the ceremonial head in the First Republic.

> **Answer**: The President (ceremonial), e.g., Nnamdi Azikiwe.

> **Answer**: Structural Adjustment Programme.

> **Explanation**: Economic policy acronym.


### Core Level


**3.4.C**: State one cause of the Civil War.

> **Answer**: Regional/ethnic tensions after coups; pogroms; failed Aburi Accord; secession move (any one).

> **Explanation**: Conflict drivers.


**3.5.C**: Name one military ruler between 1983 and 1999.

> **Answer**: Buhari, Babangida, Abacha, Abdulsalami (any one).

> **Explanation**: Leaders list.


**3.6.C**: Give one principle of the 1999 Constitution.

> **Answer**: Presidential system; federal character; separation of powers; fundamental rights (any one).

> **Explanation**: Key constitutional features.


### Challenge Level


**3.7.CH**: Explain 'No Victor, No Vanquished' policy aim.

> **Answer**: Promote reconciliation after Civil War, avoid punitive measures, reintegrate Biafra region, focus on 3Rs (Reconstruction, Rehabilitation, Reconciliation).

> **Explanation**: Post-war healing goal.


**3.8.CH**: How did June 12, 1993 annulment affect trust in government?

> **Answer**: Undermined legitimacy, sparked protests/strikes, deepened public distrust, pressured regime toward transition.

> **Explanation**: Legitimacy crisis.


**3.9.CH**: Name one contemporary governance challenge and a policy response.

> **Answer**: Security (insurgency/banditry) → military plus community policing; corruption → anti-graft agencies; economic diversification → support agriculture/tech/SMEs.

> **Explanation**: Problem-solution pairing.


**3.10.CH**: If population grows 2.5% yearly, approximate doubling time?

> **Answer**: Rule of 70: 70 / 2.5 ≈ 28 years.

> **Explanation**: Demography math.


## Glossary


**First Republic**: Nigeria's parliamentary system era 1960–1966.

- *Example*: Prime Minister Tafawa Balewa, ceremonial President Azikiwe.


**Civil War**: Nigerian conflict 1967–1970 after Biafra secession.

- *Example*: 3Rs policy after war.


**SAP**: Structural Adjustment Programme to liberalize economy and reduce state spending.

- *Example*: Introduced under Babangida.


**Federal character**: Principle to ensure diverse representation in public service.

- *Example*: Spread of appointments across states.


**Fourth Republic**: Democratic era from 1999 with presidential system.

- *Example*: Elections 1999, 2003, 2007...
